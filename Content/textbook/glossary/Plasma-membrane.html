Functions of a plasma membrane

  • A chemical is moved through a plasma membrane
  • A hydrophilic compound is blocked by a plasma membrane
  • An organic molecule is secreted by a cell to an extracellular side with a transport vesicle from cytoplasm through a plasma membrane using another organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • In receptor mediated endocytosis, directed motion — a chemical is moved in a plasma membrane through a coated pit, an extracellular side, a hydrophobic core, an inside face and an intermembrane space
  • In coated pit, transport of a chemical by means of a cell surface receptor in a eukaryotic cell to cytoplasm inside a coated vesicle from an extracellular side using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • A cell is recognized by another cell at a plasma membrane
