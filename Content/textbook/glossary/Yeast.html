Definition of yeast

Single-celled fungus that reproduces asexually by binary fission or by the pinching of small buds off a parent cell; some species exhibit cell fusion between different mating types. Yeast has plasmids and can be cultured easily and quickly.

Yeast is a type of: fungal cell, deuteromycete, and unicellular organism .

Functions of yeast

  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in yeast to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in a mitochondrion. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

Yeast also does…

  • Nutrition — yeast obtains nutrition using carbon, an organic molecule and a polymer resulting in a chemical. Here, the yeast is a decomposer.
