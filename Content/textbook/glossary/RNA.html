RNA

A type of nucleic acid consisting of nucleotide monomers with a ribose sugar and the nitrogenous bases adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G), and uracil (U); usually single-stranded; functions in protein synthesis, gene regulation, and as the genome of some viruses.

Definition of an RNA

A type of nucleic acid consisting of nucleotide monomers with a ribose sugar and the nitrogenous bases adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G), and uracil (U); usually single-stranded; functions in protein synthesis, gene regulation, and as the genome of some viruses.

The Nucleus, Chromatin, and Chromosomes (A) Chromatin consists of nuclear DNA and the proteins associated with it. When the cell is not dividing, the chromatin is dispersed throughout the nucleus. This two-dimensional image was made using a transmission electron microscope.
