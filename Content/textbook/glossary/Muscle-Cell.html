Functions of a muscle cell

  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a muscle cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • In cell signaling leading to arterial muscle relaxation, relaxation in a muscle cell with protein using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy.
  • glycogen is stored by a muscle cell

A muscle cell also does…

  • In fight or flight response, cell communication with epinephrine In muscle cell — a cell of endocrine system communicates using epinephrine with a muscle cell. Here, the muscle cell is a target cell.
