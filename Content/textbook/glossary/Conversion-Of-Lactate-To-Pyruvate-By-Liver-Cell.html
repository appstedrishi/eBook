conversion of lactate to pyruvate by liver cell

The chemical change of the excess lactate produced during lactic acid fermentation, allowing the pyruvate to enter the mitochondria in the liver cells, where oxygen is present, and complete cellular respiration.

Definition of a conversion of lactate to pyruvate by liver cell

The chemical change of the excess lactate produced during lactic acid fermentation, allowing the pyruvate to enter the mitochondria in the liver cells, where oxygen is present, and complete cellular respiration.

Conversion of lactate to pyruvate by liver cell is a type of: redox reaction.

Participants of a conversion of lactate to pyruvate by liver cell

Conversion of lactate to pyruvate by liver cell — lactate and NAD plus are converted in a liver cell to an NADH and pyruvate

Steps of conversion of lactate to pyruvate by liver cell

  • Reduction — an electron transfers from lactate to NAD plus. This process requires activation energy.
  • Oxidation — an electron transfers from lactate to NAD plus. This process requires activation energy.
