Definition of a eukaryotic cell

A type of cell with a membrane-enclosed nucleus and membrane-enclosed organelles. Organisms with eukaryotic cells (protists, plants, fungi, and animals) are called eukaryotes.

Functions of a eukaryotic cell

  • A hydrogen ion is transported by a carrier protein to a solution from an aqueous solution through a biomembrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a eukaryotic cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • A calcium ion is absorbed by 2 carrier proteins in a eukaryotic cell to an aqueous solution from cytosol through an ER membrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
