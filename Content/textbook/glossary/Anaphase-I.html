During anaphase I phase of meiosis the mitotic spindle guides the movement of chromosomes toward the poles. The sister-chromatids remain attached at their centromere and move as a single unit while the homologous chromosome moves toward the opposite pole.

Definition of an anaphase-I

During anaphase I phase of meiosis the mitotic spindle guides the movement of chromosomes toward the poles. The sister-chromatids remain attached at their centromere and move as a single unit while the homologous chromosome moves toward the opposite pole.

Anaphase-I is a type of: meiotic event.

Anaphase-I in a cell

Steps of anaphase-I

  1. 2 homologous chromosomes are separated by a kinetochore microtubule from a metaphase plate
  2. 2 sister chromatids are brought together by a mitotic spindle at a cell pole
