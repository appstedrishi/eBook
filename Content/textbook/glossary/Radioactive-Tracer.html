Radioactive tracer

radioactive tracer

A substance containing a radioactive isotope that is used to measure the speed of chemical processes and to track the movement of a substance through a natural system such as a cell or tissue. Radioactive forms of hydrogen, carbon, phosphorus, sulfur, and iodine are commonly used in applications including biochemical assays, metabolism studies, and medical diagnostics.
