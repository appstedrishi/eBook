Definition of a photosynthesis

The conversion of light energy to chemical energy that is stored in sugars or other organic compounds; occurs in plants, algae, and certain prokaryotes.

Photosynthesis is a type of: anabolic pathway.

Properties of a photosynthesis

  • Free energy change

Participants of a photosynthesis

Photosynthesis — carbon dioxide is converted in a cell to sugar. This process transforms free-energy and visible light to chemical energy.

Steps of photosynthesis

  • Light reaction — an ADP and a phosphate ion are converted in a cell with photosystem I to ATP in a thylakoid membrane. This process requires visible light.
  • Calvin cycle — ATP, carbon dioxide, a hydrogen ion, an NADPH and ribulose bisphosphate are converted in a cell to an ADP, an NADP plus, a phosphate ion and sugar. This process requires free-energy.
