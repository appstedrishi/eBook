Some functions of different types of substances

Expand All Collapse All
  • Capillary
    • blood is carried by a capillary
  • Mixture
    • In eukaryotic cell, pyruvate is converted by a multienzyme complex inside a eukaryotic cell in a eukaryote to an acetyl CoA, carbon dioxide and an NADH at cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
    • In eukaryotic cellular respiration, an ADP, an NADH, an oxygen molecule and a phosphate ion are converted by an ATP synthase in a eukaryotic cell to ATP, another NADH and a water molecule in cytoplasm. This process transforms a proton-motive force to free-energy.
  • Hydrophilic substance
    • A water molecule is attracted by a hydrophilic substance
  • Fluid
    • A mouth is protected by saliva with lysozyme
    • In chloroplast, Calvin cycle — ATP, carbon dioxide, glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, a hydrogen ion, an NADPH and ribulose bisphosphate are converted in a cell to an ADP, glucose, another glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, an NADP plus and a phosphate ion in a stroma. This process requires free-energy.
    • PH maintenance by a buffer
    • mixture is wet by saliva
  • Chemical
    • Diagnosis — disease is diagnosed with a radioactive tracer
    • A water molecule is repelled by a hydrophobic substance
