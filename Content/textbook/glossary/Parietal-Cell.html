Functions of a parietal cell

  • Active transport using ATP — a hydrogen ion is transported by means of a carrier protein to an aqueous solution from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • 2 hydrogen ions are transported by an ATP synthase and a carrier protein to a hypotonic solution from a hypertonic solution through an inner membrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a parietal cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

A parietal cell also does…

  • polypeptide and protein are secreted by a parietal cell to an extracellular side with a transport vesicle from cytoplasm through an animal plasma membrane using chemical energy, free-energy and an organic molecule. Here, the parietal cell is a secretor.
