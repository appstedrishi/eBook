Spiral cleavage

spiral cleavage

A type of embryonic development in protostomes in which the planes of cell division that transform the zygote into a ball of cells are diagonal to the vertical axis of the embryo. As a result, the cells of each tier sit in the grooves between cells of adjacent tiers.

Definition of a spiral cleavage

A type of embryonic development in protostomes in which the planes of cell division that transform the zygote into a ball of cells are diagonal to the vertical axis of the embryo. As a result, the cells of each tier sit in the grooves between cells of adjacent tiers.

Steps of spiral cleavage

  • Create consumes a microfilament and myosin and produces a contractile ring
  • Create produces 2 cleavage furrows
  • Contract in a contractile ring
  • 2 cleavage furrows grow resulting in an animal cell
