condensation reaction

Condensation reaction is a chemical reaction in which two molecules or moieties (functional groups) combine to form one single molecule, together with the loss of a small molecule.

Definition of a condensation reaction

Properties of a condensation reaction

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic reactions
      • positive with respect to an exergonic reaction

Participants of a condensation reaction

Condensation reaction consumes 2 molecules and produces another molecule. This process requires free-energy.

Types of condensation reactions

  • formation of water molecule
  • dehydration reaction
