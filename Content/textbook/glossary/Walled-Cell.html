Some functions of different types of walled cells

Expand All Collapse All
  • Plant cell
    • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a bundle-sheath cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
    • In plant mitotic cycle, cellular work in a plant cell using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy.
  • Yeast
    • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in yeast to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in a mitochondrion. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
