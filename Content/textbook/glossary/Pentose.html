Definition of a pentose

A pentose is a monosaccharide with five carbon atoms. Pentoses are organized into two groups. Aldopentoses have an aldehyde functional group at position. Ketopentoses have a ketone functional group in position 2 or 3.

Pentose is a type of: monosaccharide.
