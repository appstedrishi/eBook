Definition of compound

Compound is a chemical formed when two or more different elements combine, either through covalent or ionic bonding. A compound possesses emergent property because of the constituent elements in a fixed ratio.

Compound is a type of: chemical.

Covalent Bonding Can Form Compounds (A) Bohr models showing the formation of covalent bonds in methane, whose molecular formula is CH 4 . Electrons are shown in shells around the nucleus.
