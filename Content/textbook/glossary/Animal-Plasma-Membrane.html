Functions of an animal plasma membrane

  • 2 chemicals are moved through an animal plasma membrane through a molecular region
  • A hydrophilic compound and an oxygen molecule are blocked by an animal plasma membrane
  • In secretory cell, transport of protein in a secretory cell to an extracellular side with a transport vesicle from cytoplasm through an animal plasma membrane using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • In choanocyte, an object is transported by a choanocyte to cytoplasm inside a food vacuole from an extracellular side through an animal plasma membrane using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.

An animal plasma membrane also does…

  • The function of animal plasma membrane is blocking of a hydrophilic compound and a hydrophobic compound by an animal plasma membrane and a hydrophilic head by a hydrophilic face. Here, the animal plasma membrane is a barrier.
