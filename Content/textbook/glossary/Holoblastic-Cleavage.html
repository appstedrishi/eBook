Holoblastic cleavage

holoblastic cleavage

A type of cleavage in which there is complete division of the egg; occurs in eggs that have little yolk (such as those of the sea urchin) or a moderate amount of yolk (such as those of the frog).

Definition of a holoblastic cleavage

A type of cleavage in which there is complete division of the egg; occurs in eggs that have little yolk (such as those of the sea urchin) or a moderate amount of yolk (such as those of the frog).

Steps of holoblastic cleavage

  • Create consumes a microfilament and myosin and produces a contractile ring
  • Create produces 2 cleavage furrows
  • Contract in a contractile ring
  • 2 cleavage furrows grow resulting in an animal cell
