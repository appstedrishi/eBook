Definition of a polysaccharide

A polymer of many monosaccharides, formed by dehydration reactions.

Polysaccharide is a type of: carbohydrate and polymer.

A polysaccharide also does…

  • In chemical digestion In small intestine, hydrolysis — activation energy, a polysaccharide and a water molecule are converted to a chemical, a disaccharide and free-energy at an active site. Here, the polysaccharide is a reactant.
  • Hydrolysis — activation energy, a polysaccharide and a water molecule are converted to free-energy, maltose and another polysaccharide at an active site. Here, the first polysaccharide is a reactant.
