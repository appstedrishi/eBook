sister chromatid cohesion

Definition of a sister chromatid cohesion

During mitosis, the process in which the duplicated chromosomes (called sister chromatids) are held together by cohesin proteins into a diploid chromosome.

Sister chromatid cohesion is a type of: mitotic event.
