Definition of a cellular respiration using starch

Cellular respiration, also known as 'oxidative metabolism', is one of the key ways a cell gains useful energy.

Cellular respiration using starch is a type of: catabolic pathway and cellular respiration of complex carbohydrate .

Properties of a cellular respiration using starch

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a cellular respiration using starch

NAD plus and starch are converted by an organism to ATP, carbon dioxide, pyruvate and a water molecule. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of cellular respiration using starch

  1. Hydrolysis
  2. Glycolysis
  3. Pyruvate oxidation
  4. Citric acid cycle
  5. Oxidative phosphorylation
