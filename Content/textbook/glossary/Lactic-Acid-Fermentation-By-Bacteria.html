Properties of a lactic acid fermentation by bacteria

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a lactic acid fermentation by bacteria

Lactic acid fermentation by bacteria — an ADP, glucose and 2 NAD plus are converted in a bacterium to glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, lactate and at least 2 organic molecules (2 ATP, another glucose, an NADH, 2 pyruvate) in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Steps of lactic acid fermentation by bacteria

  • Glycolysis — 2 ADPs, glucose and NAD plus are converted in a cell to 2 ATP, another glucose, glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, lactate, another NAD plus, 2 NADHs, 2 pyruvate and a water molecule in an active site and cytosol. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • Redox reaction consumes an NADH and pyruvate and produces lactate and NAD plus
