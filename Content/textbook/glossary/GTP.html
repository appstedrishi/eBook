GTP

GTP

A molecule which is structurally and functionally very similar to ATP. This molecule stores energy in the covalent bonds between phosphate groups, which when broken releases energy and can be used to make ATP or to directly power cell processes.

Definition of a GTP

A molecule which is structurally and functionally very similar to ATP. This molecule stores energy in the covalent bonds between phosphate groups, which when broken releases energy and can be used to make ATP or to directly power cell processes.

GTP is a type of: nucleoside triphosphate .
