Functions of a glycogen phosphorylase

  • Glycogen breakdown — glycogen and an inorganic phosphate are converted in a cell to a glucose 1-phosphate, at least 2 monomers and at least 2 monosaccharides in an active site. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

A glycogen phosphorylase also does…

  • Glycogen breakdown — activation energy, glycogen and an inorganic phosphate are converted in a cell to free-energy, a glucose 1-phosphate and a monosaccharide in an active site. Here, a glycogen phosphorylase is a catalyst.
