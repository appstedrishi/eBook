The process in which proteins breakdown into their simpler components.

Properties of a protein breakdown

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a protein breakdown

Break down in a cell using protein resulting in at least 2 monomers and an organic molecule. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Steps of protein breakdown

  • Hydrolysis consumes protein and a water molecule and produces a chemical and a monomer. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
