Some functions of different types of organic molecules

Expand All Collapse All
  • Hydrocarbon molecule
    • A red light and a violet blue light are absorbed by a chlorophyll
  • Lipid
    • water and a water molecule are repelled by lipid from a hydrophobic region
  • Amino acid
    • A hydrophilic compound is repelled by an alanine from a hydrophobic region
  • Neurotransmitter
    • In cell communication between neurons, ligand binding — a neurotransmitter binds to a ligand-gated ion-channel at a receptor site
  • Protein
    • In centriole, a microtubule is attached by protein to another microtubule
  • Vitamin B1
    • A chemical is converted by a vitamin B1 to carbon dioxide and another chemical. This process requires energy.
  • Polysaccharide
    • In cellulose digestion, a cell is supported by cellulose
    • In cellulose digestion, a water molecule is attracted by cellulose
    • energy is stored by glycogen
  • Fatty acid
    • Repel by a fatty acid
  • Citrate
    • A protein enzyme is inhibit by citrate in a cell
  • RNA
    • A genetic code is transmitted by an MRNA
    • An MRNA is protected by a five prime cap
  • Nucleotide
    • In energy investment phase of glycolysis, phosphofructokinase is regulated by an AMP
    • In biological process, chemical energy is stored by ATP
    • In fibronectin, attach — ATP is attached to fibronectin at an inside face
  • Hormone
    • In cell communication with ethylene, ligand binding — ethylene binds to a receptor protein at a receptor site
  • Inositol Trisphosphate
    • In release of calcium ion by endoplasmic reticulum, ligand binding — Inositol Trisphosphate binds to gated Channel
  • Enzyme
    • Chemical reaction consumes a chemical and produces another chemical
  • Pigment
    • A blue green light and a violet light are absorbed by a carotenoid through a carbon skeleton
  • Dinucleotide
    • In prokaryotic oxidative phosphorylation, oxidation — an electron transfers from a FADH2 to another FADH2. This process requires activation energy.
    • In cellular respiration of fat, reduction — an electron transfers from a FADH2 to a chemical
    • In metabolic pathway, an electron is carried by a FADH2
  • Pyruvate
    • In lactic acid fermentation, oxidation — an electron transfers from a chemical to pyruvate. This process requires activation energy.
