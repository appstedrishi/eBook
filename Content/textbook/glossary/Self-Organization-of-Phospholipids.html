Self organization of phospholipids

self organization of phospholipids

Self organization of phospholipids is the process in which phospholipids orient themselves with respect to the water and with each other so that the hydrophilic ("water loving") head groups are grouped together and face the water, and the hydrophobic ("water fearing") tails turn away from the water and toward each other.
