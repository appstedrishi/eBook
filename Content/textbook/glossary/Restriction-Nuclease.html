Functions of a restriction nuclease

  • restriction site is recognized by a restriction nuclease
  • foreign DNA and a water molecule are converted by a restriction nuclease to 2 chemicals at an active site. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • foreign DNA is cut by a restriction nuclease using a water molecule resulting in at least 2 sticky ends at restriction site

A restriction nuclease also does…

  • activation energy, foreign DNA and a water molecule are converted by a restriction nuclease to 2 chemicals and free-energy at an active site. Here, the restriction nuclease is a catalyst.
