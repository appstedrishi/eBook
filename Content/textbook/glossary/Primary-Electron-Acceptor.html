Primary electron acceptor

primary electron acceptor

In the thylakoid membrane of a chloroplast or in the membrane of some prokaryotes, a specialized molecule that shares the reaction-center complex with a pair of chlorophyll a molecules and that accepts an electron from them.

Definition of a primary electron acceptor

In the thylakoid membrane of a chloroplast or in the membrane of some prokaryotes, a specialized molecule that shares the reaction-center complex with a pair of chlorophyll a molecules and that accepts an electron from them.

Primary electron acceptor is a type of: electron recipient .

Examples of a primary electron acceptor

  • In redox reaction during linear electron flow, an electron transfers from a P680 to a molecule. Here, the molecule is a primary electron acceptor.
  • In redox reaction during cyclic electron flow, an electron transfers from a P700 to a molecule. Here, the molecule is a primary electron acceptor.
