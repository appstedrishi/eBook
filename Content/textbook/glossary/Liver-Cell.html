Functions of a liver cell

  • 2 hydrogen ions are transported by an ATP synthase and a carrier protein to a hypotonic solution from a hypertonic solution through an inner membrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a liver cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • alcohol, a barbiturate and a drug are detoxified by a liver cell using a protein enzyme

A liver cell also does…

  • M-phase consumes ATP and produces a eukaryotic cell and a liver cell. Here, another liver cell is a parent cell.
  • Cell communication with epinephrine In liver cell — a cell of endocrine system communicates using epinephrine with a liver cell. Here, the liver cell is a target cell.
