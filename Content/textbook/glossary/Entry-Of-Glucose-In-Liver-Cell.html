Steps of entry of glucose In liver cell

  • Active transport using ATP — glucose and a hydrophilic compound are transported by means of a cotransporter to an aqueous solution from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • Facilitated diffusion using carrier protein — a chemical and a hydrophilic compound diffuse by means of a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a cotransporter from a hypertonic solution through a biomembrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
