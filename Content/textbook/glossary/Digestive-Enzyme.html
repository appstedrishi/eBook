Functions of a digestive enzyme

  • Hydrolysis consumes a carbohydrate and a water molecule and produces a chemical and sugar. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Some functions of different types of digestive enzymes

Expand All Collapse All
  • Cellulase
    • Chemical reaction — cellulose and a water molecule are converted in a cell for an organism to glucose. This process requires activation energy and potential energy.
  • Amylase
    • Hydrolysis of starch — starch and a water molecule are converted in a cell to at least 2 monomers and at least 2 monosaccharides at an active site. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

A digestive enzyme also does…

  • Hydrolysis — activation energy, a chemical and a water molecule are converted to another 2 chemicals and free-energy at an active site. Here, a digestive enzyme is a catalyst.
