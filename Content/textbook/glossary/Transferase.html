Functions of a transferase

  • Synthesis of an RNA, an RNA primer and a water molecule by a transcription factor in a cell and a transcription unit toward a 3 prime end in another cell using a gene from a 5 prime end using 2 monomers in an active site. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • A DNA is copied by a DNA ligase, a DNA polymerase III and an RNA polymerase in a cell to a 5 prime end from a 3 prime end using 2 monomers resulting in another DNA and a water molecule. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • Glycogen breakdown — glycogen and an inorganic phosphate are converted in a cell to a glucose 1-phosphate, at least 2 monomers and at least 2 monosaccharides in an active site. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • Substrate level phosphorylation — an ADP and phosphoenolpyruvate are converted in a cell to ATP and pyruvate in an active site. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • Phosphorylation — a molecule is converted to a chemical in an active site
