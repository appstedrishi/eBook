Figure 1.8
The Blueprint of Life Using technologies that reveal the sequence of nucleotides that make up the genome of an organism, we can detect small alterations that are responsible for specific traits. The EDAR gene is involved in hair development, and a single nucleotide substitution results in a single amino acid difference between the EDAR proteins of humans with Asian origins and those with African or European origins.
Figure 1.6
DNA Is Life s Blueprint The instructions for life are contained in the sequences of nucleotides in DNA molecules. Specific DNA nucleotide sequences compose genes. The average length of a single human gene is 16,000 nucleotides. The information in each gene provides the cell with the information it needs to manufacture molecules of a specific protein.
