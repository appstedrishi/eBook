A member of the eukaryotic kingdom that includes organisms that absorb nutrients after decomposing organic material.

Some functions of different types of fungi

Expand All Collapse All
  • Yeast
    • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in yeast to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in a mitochondrion. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

Fungus also does…

  • Nutrition — fungus obtains nutrition using carbon, an organic molecule and a polymer resulting in a chemical. Here, the fungus is a decomposer.
