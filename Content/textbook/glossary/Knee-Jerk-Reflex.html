Knee jerk reflex

knee jerk reflex

A simple reflex arc in which the sudden, unexpected stretching of the tendon attached to the quadriceps is sensed by a specialized sensory neuron in the quadriceps. This sensory neuron signals a motor neuron to quickly counter the stretch by commanding the quadriceps to contract, causing your leg to jerk forward slightly.
