Definition of a fibroblast

A type of cell in loose connective tissue that secretes the protein ingredients of the extracellular fibers.

Fibroblast is a type of: animal cell.

  • Diameter
    • inherent to all eukaryotic cells
      • between 10 and 100 micrometers
  • Size
    • inherent to all eukaryotic cells
      • big with respect to a prokaryote
  • Structural complexity
    • inherent to all cells
      • simple with respect to an organism
  • Has region
    • 2 cell poles
    • inherent to all animal cells
      • cell pole

    • inherent to all cells

      • surface
  • Has part

    • inherent to all animal cells

        An animal plasma membrane is outside cytoplasm

      • centrosome

        A centrosome is near a nucleus

      • peroxisome

        A peroxisome is inside cytosol

Functions of a fibroblast

  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a fibroblast to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

A fibroblast also does…

  • In effect of PDGF on cell division, cell division — a fibroblast is divided using ATP resulting in a cell. Here, the fibroblast is a parent cell.
