Participants of a terrestrial nitrogen cycle

Terrestrial nitrogen cycle — a nitrogen molecule undergoes the terrestrial nitrogen cycle in soil in an ecosystem

Steps of terrestrial nitrogen cycle

  • A plant is consumed by an organism
  • Fixation in a factory using a nitrogen molecule resulting in a nitrogenous compound
  • A nitric oxide gas is released by a factory to atmosphere
  • A nitrogenous compound is added to soil
  • An amino acid is taken in by a root hair to an epidermal cell from soil
  • A soil solution is taken in by a root hair to an epidermal cell from soil
  • Chemical reaction consumes a nitrate ion and produces an ammonium ion
  • Nitrogen fixation — a nitrogen fixing bacterium fixes in soil using a nitrogen molecule resulting in ammonia
  • Decomposition — fungus, an organism and a prokaryote decompose a dead organism resulting in an inorganic molecule and an organic molecule
  • Ammonification — detritus undergoes ammonification by an ammonifying bacterium, fungus and an organism using an organic molecule resulting in ammonia and another organic molecule
  • Chemical reaction consumes ammonia and a hydrogen ion and produces an ammonium ion
  • Nitrification — a nitrifying bacterium nitrifications using a chemical resulting in another chemical
