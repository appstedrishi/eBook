A molecule also does…

  • A transport protein is transported by a molecule. Here, the molecule is an inhibitor.
  • Isomerization reaction — a molecule is converted to another molecule in an active site. Here, the first molecule is a reactant.
  • Carbon fixation — ATP, activation energy and a molecule are converted in a cell resulting in an organic molecule at an active site. Here, the molecule is a reactant.
  • In cholera process, synthesis of cAMP — ATP, free-energy and a molecule are converted in an animal cell to cyclic AMP, another free-energy and pyrophosphate at an active site. Here, the molecule is a reactant.
  • In signal transduction during cell signaling with cAMP and G-protein coupled receptor, ATP, free-energy and a molecule are converted in a cell to cyclic AMP, another free-energy and pyrophosphate at an active site and cytoplasm. Here, the molecule is a reactant.
  • In signal transduction during cell communication with epinephrine In liver cell, ATP, free-energy and a molecule are converted in a liver cell to cyclic AMP, another free-energy and pyrophosphate at an active site and cytoplasm. Here, the molecule is a reactant.
  • In redox reaction during linear electron flow, an electron transfers from a P680 to a molecule. Here, the molecule is an oxidizing agent.
  • In redox reaction during linear electron flow, an electron transfers from a P680 to a molecule. Here, the molecule is a primary electron acceptor.
  • In redox reaction during cyclic electron flow, an electron transfers from a P700 to a molecule. Here, the molecule is a primary electron acceptor.
  • In redox reaction during cyclic electron flow, an electron transfers from a P700 to a molecule. Here, the molecule is an oxidizing agent.
  • In redox reaction during cyclic electron flow, an electron transfers from a molecule to ferredoxin. Here, the molecule is a reducing agent.
  • In competitive inhibition, compete — 2 molecules compete at a receptor site. Here, one of the molecules is a contestant.
  • In signal transduction during cell communication with epinephrine In liver cell, a glycogen phosphorylase and a molecule are converted to another glycogen phosphorylase in an active site. Here, the molecule is a reactant.
