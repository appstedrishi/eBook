citric acid cycle In eukaryote

A chemical cycle, occuring in the mitochondrion of eukaryotes, involving eight steps that completes the metabolic breakdown of glucose molecules begun in glycolysis by oxidizing acetyl CoA to carbon dioxide; the second major stage in cellular respiration.

Steps of citric acid cycle In eukaryote

  1. Synthesis reaction consumes an acetyl CoA and oxaloacetate and produces citrate and coenzyme A
  2. Isomerization reaction consumes citrate and produces an isocitrate
  3. Chemical reaction consumes an isocitrate and NAD plus and produces an alpha ketoglutarate, carbon dioxide and an NADH
  4. Chemical reaction consumes an alpha ketoglutarate, coenzyme A and NAD plus and produces carbon dioxide, an NADH and a succinyl CoA
  5. Chemical reaction consumes a succinyl CoA and produces ATP and a succinate
  6. Redox reaction consumes an FAD and a succinate and produces a FADH2 and a fumarate
  7. Synthesis reaction consumes a fumarate and a water molecule and produces a malate
