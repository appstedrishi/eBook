Definition of a synthesis of glycoprotein

The formation of a protein with a carbohydrate component. The addition of the small carbohydrate molecules to proteins occurs in the rough ER during protein synthesis.

Synthesis of glycoprotein is a type of: synthesis of protein .

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic reactions
      • positive with respect to an exergonic reaction

Participants of a synthesis of glycoprotein

2 monomers are converted by a bound ribosome in a eukaryotic cell to a glycoprotein and a water molecule at an active site. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.

Steps of synthesis of glycoprotein

  • Translation — a bound ribosome, a protein enzyme and a TRNA convert a glycoprotein, polypeptide and a water molecule in a eukaryotic cell from an MRNA using a GTP and 3 monomers at a rough endoplasmic reticulum. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy and thermal energy.
  • A monomer is attached by a protein enzyme to protein at a rough endoplasmic reticulum
  • A glycoprotein is moved in a rough endoplasmic reticulum
  • Post translational modification — a glycoprotein and polypeptide are modified resulting in a functional protein in a rough endoplasmic reticulum
  • Dehydration reaction — a GTP and 3 monomers are converted to a glycoprotein, polypeptide and a water molecule in a bound ribosome and a rough endoplasmic reticulum. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy and thermal energy.
  • A glycoprotein is moved in Golgi apparatus
  • Change occurs in Golgi apparatus
