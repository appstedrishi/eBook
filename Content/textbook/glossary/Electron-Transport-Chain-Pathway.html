Electron transport chain pathway

electron transport chain pathway

The process in which energy is released from electron carriers in a stepwise manner through a series of redox reactions along an electron transport chain. The released energy is used to pump protons across a membrane, resulting in a proton motive force which can perform cellular work.

Definition of an electron transport chain pathway

The process in which energy is released from electron carriers in a stepwise manner through a series of redox reactions along an electron transport chain. The released energy is used to pump protons across a membrane, resulting in a proton motive force which can perform cellular work.

Electron transport chain pathway is a type of: metabolic pathway .

Participants of an electron transport chain pathway

Transfering energy in a cell from a FADH2 and an NADH (2 reactants) to a chemical. This process releases a proton-motive force.

Steps of electron transport chain pathway

  • Electron transport chain reaction — an electron transport chain converts a FADH2 to a chemical. This process releases free-energy and a proton-motive force.
  • Electron transport chain reaction — an electron transport chain converts an NADH to a chemical. This process releases free-energy and a proton-motive force.
