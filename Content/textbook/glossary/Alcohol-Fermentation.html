Properties of an alcohol fermentation

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of an alcohol fermentation

Alcohol fermentation — glucose is converted in a cell to carbon dioxide and at least 2 organic molecules (2 ADPs, 2 ATP, ethyl alcohol, 2 glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, an NADH). This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Steps of alcohol fermentation

  1. Glycolysis — 2 ADPs, glucose and NAD plus are converted in a cell to 2 ATP, 2 NADHs, 2 pyruvate and a water molecule in cytosol. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  2. Decarboxylation consumes a fructose 1, 6 bisphosphate and pyruvate and produces acetaldehyde, carbon dioxide, a dihydroxyacetone phosphate and glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate. This process requires energy.
  3. Redox reaction consumes acetaldehyde and an NADH and produces ethyl alcohol and NAD plus

Types of alcohol fermentations

  • alcohol fermentation by yeast
  • alcohol fermentation by bacteria

Fermentation Glycolysis produces pyruvate, ATP, and NADH from glucose. Lactic acid fermentation uses NADH as a reducing agent to reduce pyruvate to lactate, thus regenerating NAD + to keep glycolysis operating. In alcoholic fermentation, pyruvate from glycolysis is converted into acetaldehyde, and CO 2 is released. NADH from glycolysis is used to reduce acetaldehyde to ethanol, thus regenerating NAD + to keep glycolysis operating.
