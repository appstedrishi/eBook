Irreversible non competitive inhibition

irreversible non competitive inhibition

Non-competitive inhibition is a type of enzyme inhibition where the inhibitor reversibly binds to an allosteric site, a site different than the active site, on the enzyme, preventing the substrate from being able to bind to the active site. If the inhibitor attaches to the enzyme by covalent bonds, inhibition is usually irreversible.
