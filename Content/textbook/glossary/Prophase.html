Definition of a prophase

The first stage of mitosis, in which the chromatin condenses, the mitotic spindle begins to form, and the nucleolus disappears, but the nucleus remains intact.

Prophase is a type of: mitotic stage.

Steps of prophase

  1. Condense — a eukaryotic cell condenses a diploid chromosome to a condensed diploid chromosome
  2. A nucleolus is disappeared in a eukaryotic cell
  3. Sister chromatid cohesion in a cell
  4. Mitotic spindle formation — a maturation-promoting-factor mitotics spindle formation in a eukaryotic cell resulting in a mitotic spindle in 2 centrosomes and cytoplasm
  5. 2 centrosomes are separated from a eukaryotic cell from a nucleus
