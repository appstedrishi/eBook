Definition of an electron microscope

A microscope that uses magnets to focus an electron beam on or through a specimen, resulting in resolving power a thousandfold greater than that of a light microscope. A transmission electron microscope (TEM) is used to study the internal structure of thin sections of cells. A scanning electron microscope (SEM) is used to study the fine details of cell surfaces.

Cells Are Building Blocks for Life These photographs were taken with electron microscopes (see Figure 5.3) and enhanced with added color to highlight details. (A) Liposomes are tiny round bubbles known as vesicles and are made out of the same material as a cell membrane. Liposomes can be filled with drugs and used to deliver drugs to cells.
