Fibrous protein

fibrous protein

Proteins that tend to be insoluble and strong and so play a structural role in organisms for support or protection. Fibrous proteins can be subdivided into several different types: 1.keratins, found in hair, fingernails, and bird feathers 2. collagens the most abundant proteins in a vertebrate body found in connective tissues such as cartilage 3. elastins, found in ligaments, around blood vessels.
