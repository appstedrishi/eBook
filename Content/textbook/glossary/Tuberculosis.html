Tuberculosis

tuberculosis

An infectious disease caused by various strains of mycobacteria. Tuberculosis usually attacks the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. It is spread through the air, when people who have the disease cough, sneeze, or spit.

Definition of tuberculosis

An infectious disease caused by various strains of mycobacteria. Tuberculosis usually attacks the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. It is spread through the air, when people who have the disease cough, sneeze, or spit.
