Participants of a cellular respiration

Cellular respiration in a cell using glucose and 6 oxygen molecules resulting in 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, an NADH and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of cellular respiration

  1. Glycolysis — 2 ADPs, an acetyl CoA, glucose and 2 NAD plus are converted in a cell to 2 ATP, carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 2 pyruvate in cytosol. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  2. Pyruvate oxidation — a multienzyme complex converts pyruvate in a cell to an acetyl CoA, carbon dioxide and an NADH. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  3. Citric acid cycle — an acetyl CoA, 3 NAD plus and oxaloacetate are converted in a cell to ATP, 2 carbon dioxide, a FADH2 and 3 NADHs. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  4. Oxidative phosphorylation — an ATP synthase converts an ADP, an oxygen molecule and a phosphate ion in a cell to ATP, an NADH and a water molecule. This process transforms a proton-motive force to free-energy.

Types of cellular respirations

  • prokaryotic cellular respiration
  • eukaryotic cellular respiration
