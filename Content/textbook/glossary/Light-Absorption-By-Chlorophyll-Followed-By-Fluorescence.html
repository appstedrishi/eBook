light absorption by chlorophyll followed by fluorescence

The trapping of light energy is the key to photosynthesis. The first event is the absorption of light by a photoreceptor molecule. The principal photoreceptor in the chloroplasts of most green plants is chlorophyll a, a substituted tetrapyrrole. The four nitrogen atoms of the pyrroles are coordinated to a magnesium ion. Unlike a porphyrin such as heme, chlorophyll has a reduced pyrrole ring. Another distinctive feature of chlorophyll is the presence of phytol, a highly hydrophobic 20-carbon alcohol, esterified to an acid side chain.
