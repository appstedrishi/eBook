Properties of a synthesis of protein

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic reactions
      • positive with respect to an exergonic reaction

Participants of a synthesis of protein

Synthesis of protein — 2 monomers are converted in a cell to protein and a water molecule. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.

Steps of synthesis of protein

  • Translation — a ribosome and a TRNA convert polypeptide and a water molecule in a cell from an MRNA using an amino acid, a GTP and 2 monomers. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy and thermal energy.
  • Dehydration reaction consumes 2 monomers and produces protein and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
  • Post translational modification — polypeptide is modified resulting in a functional protein
