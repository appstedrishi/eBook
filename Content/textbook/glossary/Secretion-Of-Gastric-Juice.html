Steps of secretion of gastric juice

  • Active transport using ATP — a hydrogen ion is transported by means of a carrier protein to an aqueous solution from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • Facilitated diffusion — a chemical diffuses to an aqueous solution using a transport protein from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
  • Mix consumes hydrochloric acid and pepsin and produces gastric juice
  • Secretion — an animate thing secretes pepsinogen
  • pepsinogen is activated by a hydrochloric acid molecule resulting in pepsin and polypeptide
  • pepsinogen is activated by pepsin resulting in another pepsin
  • Synthesis reaction consumes a hydrogen ion and produces a hydrochloric acid molecule
