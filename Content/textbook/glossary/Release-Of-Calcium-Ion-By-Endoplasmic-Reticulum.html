release of calcium ion by endoplasmic reticulum

Definition of a release of calcium ion by endoplasmic reticulum

Ca2+ ions are actively transported and stored within the ER lumen. The release of the ions back into the cytosol is triggered by a signal, which in turn triggers various responses depending on what kind of cell it is.

Release of calcium ion by endoplasmic reticulum is a type of: facilitated diffusion using channel protein and facilitated diffusion of ion .

Steps of release of calcium ion by endoplasmic reticulum

  1. Ligand binding
  2. A calcium ion is moved by gated Channel through an ER membrane to a hypotonic solution from a hypertonic solution through a hydrophilic face and a hydrophobic core
  3. A calcium ion is released by a smooth endoplasmic reticulum
