Q. What are the differences between chloroplasts and mitochondria?
A.
ChloroplastMitochondrion
definition

An organelle found in plants and photosynthetic protists that absorbs sunlight and uses it to drive the synthesis of organic compounds from carbon dioxide and water.

An organelle in eukaryotic cells that serves as the site of cellular respiration.

Figure 6.18 The chloroplast, site of photosynthesis. (a) Many plants have disk-shaped choloroplasts, as shown here. A typical chloroplast has three compartments: the intermembrane space, the stroma, and the thylakoid space. Free ribosomes are present in the stroma, as are copies of chloroplast DNA molecules. (b) This fluorescence micrograph shows a cell of the green alga Spirogyra crassa , which is named for its spiral chloroplasts. Under natural light the chloroplasts appear green, but under ultraviolet light they naturally fluoresce red, as shown here.

Figure 10.8 Why leaves are green: interaction of light with chloroplasts.
The chlorophyll molecules of chloroplasts absorb violet-blue and red light (the colors most effective in driving photosynthesis) and reflect or transmit green light. This is why leaves appear green.
