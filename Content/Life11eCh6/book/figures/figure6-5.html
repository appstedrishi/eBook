Original Paper: Frye, L. D. and M. Edidin. 1970. The rapid intermixing of cell surface antigens after formation of mouse-human heterokaryons. Journal of Cell Science 7: 319â€“335.

Two animal cells can be fused together in the laboratory, forming a single large cell (heterokaryon). This phenomenon was used to test whether membrane proteins can diffuse independently in the plane of the cell membrane.

HYPOTHESIS Proteins embedded in a membrane can diffuse freely within the membrane.

METHOD

CONCLUSION Membrane proteins can diffuse rapidly in the plane of the membrane.
