Figure 7.3 A Signal and Its Receptor

(A) The adenosine 2A receptor occurs in the human brain, where it is involved in inhibiting active wakefulness. (B) Adenosine is the normal (endogenous) ligand for the receptor. Caffeine has a structure similar to that of adenosine and can act as an antagonist that binds the receptor and prevents its normal functioning.
