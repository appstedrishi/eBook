Definition of a mesophyll cell

In C4 plants, a type of loosely arranged photosynthetic cell located between the bundle sheath and the leaf surface.

Mesophyll cell is a type of: parenchyma cell.

Functions of a mesophyll cell

  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a mesophyll cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs, pyruvate and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • In C4-photosynthesis, transfer of malate to bundle sheath cell — a malate is transferred to a bundle-sheath cell from a mesophyll cell through a plasmodesmata
  • Crassulacean acid metabolism — a CAM plant and a lithops perform crassulacean acid metabolism in a mesophyll cell using carbon dioxide and a water molecule resulting in glucose and an oxygen molecule in a chloroplast and cytoplasm. This process transforms free-energy and visible light to chemical energy.
  • In crassulacean acid metabolism, an organic acid is stored by a mesophyll cell in a CAM plant and a central vacuole
