Participants of a synthesis of mRNA In eukaryote

Synthesis of mRNA In eukaryote — 2 monomers are converted in a eukaryotic cell to an MRNA and a water molecule in a nucleus. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.

Steps of synthesis of mRNA In eukaryote

  • Transcription by a gene by an RNA polymerase and a transcription factor in a eukaryotic cell and a transcription unit to a 3 prime end from a 5 prime end using ATP and 2 monomers resulting in an RNA and a water molecule in a nucleus. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • RNA processing — pre-mRNA is processed in a cell resulting in an MRNA in a nucleus
  • An MRNA is moved to cytoplasm out of a nuclear matrix through a nuclear pore
  • Dehydration reaction consumes 2 monomers and produces an MRNA and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
