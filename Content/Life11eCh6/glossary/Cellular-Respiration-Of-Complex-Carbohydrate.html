Definition of a cellular respiration of complex carbohydrate

The catabolic pathways of aerobic respiration, which break down complex carbohydrates and use an electron transport chain for the production of ATP.

    • inherent to all exergonic reactions

Participants of a cellular respiration of complex carbohydrate

Cellular respiration of complex carbohydrate — an organism performs cellular respiration in another organism using a carbohydrate resulting in ATP, carbon dioxide and a water molecule. This process releases free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of cellular respiration of complex carbohydrate

  1. Hydrolysis consumes a carbohydrate and a water molecule and produces an organic molecule and another water molecule. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  2. Glycolysis — 2 ADPs, glucose and 2 NAD plus are converted in a cell to 2 ATP, 2 NADHs and 2 pyruvate in cytosol. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  3. Pyruvate oxidation — a multienzyme complex converts pyruvate in a cell to an acetyl CoA, carbon dioxide and an NADH. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  4. Citric acid cycle — an acetyl CoA, 3 NAD plus and oxaloacetate are converted in a cell to ATP, 2 carbon dioxide, a FADH2 and 3 NADHs. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  5. Oxidative phosphorylation — an ATP synthase converts an ADP and a phosphate ion in a cell to ATP and a water molecule. This process transforms a proton-motive force to free-energy.
