weak acid

Definition of a weak acid

An acid that does not dissociate completely. It does not release all of its hydrogens in a solution, donating only a partial amount of its protons to the solution. Examples of weak acids include acetic acid (CH3COOH) and oxalic acid (H2C2O4).
