Properties of a combustion of fossil fuel

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a combustion of fossil fuel

A hydrocarbon molecule and an oxygen molecule are converted by a person to carbon dioxide, nitric oxide, a sulfur dioxide and a water molecule. This process releases free-energy.

Steps of combustion of fossil fuel

  • Reduction — an electron transfers from a hydrocarbon molecule to an oxygen molecule. This process requires activation energy.
  • Oxidation — an electron transfers from a hydrocarbon molecule to an oxygen molecule. This process requires activation energy.
