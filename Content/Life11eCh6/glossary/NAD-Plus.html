Definition of NAD plus

A coenzyme that acts as an oxidizing agent by accepting energized electrons during glycolysis and cellular respiration.

NAD plus is a type of: dinucleotide.

The Citric Acid Cycle The citric acid cycle has eight steps; in the last step, the starting material acceptor, oxaloacetate, is regenerated. Energy is released and captured by reducing NAD + or FAD, or by producing GTP. 6C, 5C, and so on indicate the number of carbon atoms in each intermediate in the cycle.
Figure 9.5
Glycolysis Converts Glucose into Pyruvate Glucose is converted to pyruvate in ten enzyme-catalyzed steps. Along the way, two ATP are used (Steps 1 and 3), two NAD + are reduced to two NADH (Step 6), and four ATP are produced (Steps 7 and 10).
Figure 9.3
NAD + /NADH Is an Electron Carrier in Redox Reactions NAD + is an important electron acceptor in redox reactions, and thus its reduced form, NADH, is an important energy intermediary in cells. Most of the molecule (left) remains unchanged by the redox reaction.
