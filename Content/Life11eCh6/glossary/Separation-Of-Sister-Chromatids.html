Definition of a separation of sister chromatids

During mitosis, the sequence of events which results in the separation of the sister chromatids which make up a diploid chromosome into two separate and identical chromosomes.

Separation of sister chromatids is a type of: mitotic event.

Chromatid Attachment and Separation The cohesin protein complex holds sister chromatids together at the centromere. The enzyme separase hydrolyzes cohesin at the end of metaphase, allowing the chromatids to separate into daughter chromosomes.
