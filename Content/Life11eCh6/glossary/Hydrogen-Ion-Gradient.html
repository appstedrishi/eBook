A hydrogen ion gradient is a measurement of how the concentration of hydrogen ions change from one place to another, across a membrane.

Definition of a hydrogen ion gradient

A hydrogen ion gradient is a measurement of how the concentration of hydrogen ions change from one place to another, across a membrane.

Hydrogen ion gradient is a type of: electrochemical gradient .

Photophosphorylation In the thylakoid membrane, electrons are passed from photosystem II to photosystem I via a series of electron carriers, including plastoquinone (PQ), cytochrome (Cyt), and plastocyanin (PC). From photosystem I the electrons are passed to ferredoxin (Fd) and then to NADP + reductase. This process results in a proton gradient across the membrane, which drives ATP synthesis. Properties of a hydrogen ion gradient

  • Quantity
    • The quantity of the hydrogen ion gradient is directly proportional to the quantity of the proton-motive force.

Structure of a hydrogen ion gradient

  • Possesses
    • proton-motive force
    • inherent to all electrochemical gradients
      • membrane potential
  • Has part
    • inherent to all electrochemical gradients
      • concentration gradient

        A concentration gradient is between 2 solutions

Functions of a hydrogen ion gradient

  • An ADP and an inorganic phosphate are converted by an ATP synthase with a hydrogen ion gradient to ATP and free-energy
