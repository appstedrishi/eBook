Participants of a generation of hydrogen ion gradient across biomembrane

A hydrogen ion is transported by a carrier protein to a solution from an aqueous solution through a biomembrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.

Steps of generation of hydrogen ion gradient across biomembrane

  • Consume of chemical energy
  • thermal energy is released by an organic molecule
  • A hydrogen ion is moved by a carrier protein through a biomembrane to a solution from an aqueous solution through a hydrophobic core
