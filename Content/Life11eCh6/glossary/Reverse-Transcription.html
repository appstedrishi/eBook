Definition of a reverse transcription

A genetic process whereby RNA is used as the template for producing DNA.

Reverse transcription is a type of: copy and synthesis of DNA strand .

Properties of a reverse transcription

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic reactions
      • positive with respect to an exergonic reaction

Participants of a reverse transcription

A DNA polymerase and reverse transcriptase reverse transcription an RNA (a template) in a cell using a deoxyribonucleoside monophosphate and 2 monomers resulting in a DNA strand and a water molecule in a replication fork. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.

Steps of reverse transcription

  • RNA priming in a cell using 2 monomers resulting in an RNA, an RNA primer and a water molecule. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • deoxyribonucleoside triphosphate is attached by a DNA polymerase to a DNA at a 3 prime end. This process requires energy.
  • pyrophosphate is broken by a DNA polymerase resulting in a phosphate group. This process releases energy.
  • Dehydration reaction consumes a deoxyribonucleoside monophosphate and a monomer and produces a DNA strand and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
