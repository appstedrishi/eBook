Definition of lysozyme

An enzyme that destroys bacterial cell walls; in mammals, found in sweat, tears, and saliva.

Lysozyme is a type of: hydrolase.

Functions of lysozyme

  • Hydrolysis — a chemical and a water molecule are converted to another 2 chemicals at an active site. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • lysozyme is attached to a cell wall at an active site

Lysozyme also does…

  • Hydrolysis — activation energy, a chemical and a water molecule are converted to another 2 chemicals and free-energy at an active site. Here, lysozyme is a catalyst.
  • Destroy — a cell wall is destroyed resulting in an organic structure. Here, lysozyme is an antibacterial agent.
