Chlorophyll

chlorophyll

A green pigment located within the chloroplasts of plants and algae and in the membranes of certain prokaryotes. Chlorophyll a participates directly in the light reactions, which convert solar energy to chemical energy.

Definition of a chlorophyll

A green pigment located within the chloroplasts of plants and algae and in the membranes of certain prokaryotes. Chlorophyll a participates directly in the light reactions, which convert solar energy to chemical energy.

Chlorophyll is a type of: photosynthetic pigment and porphyrin.

(B) This simplified illustration of the entire photosystem uses chlorophyll molecules to represent the lightharvesting complexes. Energy from a photon is transferred from one pigment molecule to another, until it reaches a chlorophyll a molecule in the reaction center.
