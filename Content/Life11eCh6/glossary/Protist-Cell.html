Definition of a protist cell

The cell of any eukaryote that is not a plant, animal, or fungus. They are usually unicellular though some are colonial and others are multicellular.

Functions of a protist cell

  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a unicellular organism to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
