Participants of a eukaryotic translation

2 GTPs and a monomer are converted by a ribosome and a TRNA using an MRNA to polypeptide and a water molecule in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of eukaryotic translation

  • Translation initiation — a GTP and an initiation factor initiate translation in a cell resulting in a translation initiation complex in a ribosome. This process transforms free-energy to thermal energy.
  • Translation elongation — an amino acid and a GTP are converted in a cell and an MRNA using an elongation factor, a ribosome and a TRNA resulting in polypeptide at another ribosome. This process transforms free-energy to thermal energy.
  • Translation termination — a stop codon translations termination in a cell and an MRNA with a release factor using an amino acid and a GTP resulting in polypeptide in a ribosome. This process transforms free-energy to thermal energy.
  • Dehydration reaction consumes a GTP and a monomer and produces polypeptide and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
