Functions of a vertebrate cell

  • A hydrogen ion is transported by a carrier protein to a solution from an aqueous solution through a biomembrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a vertebrate cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
