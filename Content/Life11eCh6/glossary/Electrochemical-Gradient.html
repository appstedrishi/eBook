electrochemical gradient

The diffusion gradient of an ion, which is affected by both the concentration difference of the ion across a membrane (a chemical force) and the ion's tendency to move relative to the membrane potential (an electrical force).

Definition of an electrochemical gradient

The diffusion gradient of an ion, which is affected by both the concentration difference of the ion across a membrane (a chemical force) and the ion's tendency to move relative to the membrane potential (an electrical force).

Functions of an electrochemical gradient

  • A potassium ion and a sodium ion are transported by a cell by an electrochemical gradient through a biomembrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy.
