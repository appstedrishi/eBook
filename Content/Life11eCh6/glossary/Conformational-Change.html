Conformational change

conformational change

The alteration in the shape of a protein as a result of change in the environment (pH, temperature, ionic strength) or the binding of a ligand to a receptor or the binding of substrate to an enzyme.

Definition of a conformational change

The alteration in the shape of a protein as a result of change in the environment (pH, temperature, ionic strength) or the binding of a ligand to a receptor or the binding of substrate to an enzyme.

Conformational change is a type of: change of shape.
