Properties of a facilitated diffusion of hydrated ions

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all spontaneous changes
      • negative with respect to a non spontaneous change
  • Rate
    • slow with respect to a move

Participants of a facilitated diffusion of hydrated ions

A hydrated ion is diffused by a concentration gradient in a cell to a hypotonic solution with a channel protein from a hypertonic solution through a plasma membrane using a membrane potential in a hydrophobic core. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.

Steps of facilitated diffusion of hydrated ions

  • A hydrated ion and an oxygen molecule are moved by a channel protein through a plasma membrane to a hypotonic solution from a hypertonic solution through a cytoplasmic side and a hydrophobic core
