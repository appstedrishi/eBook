Definition of a prometaphase

The second stage of mitosis, in which discrete chromosomes consisting of identical sister chromatids appear, the nuclear envelope fragments, and the spindle microtubules attach to the kinetochores of the chromosomes.

Prometaphase is a type of: mitotic stage.

Prometaphase in a eukaryotic cell resulting in a cell

Steps of prometaphase

  • 2 centrosomes are separated from a cell
  • Phosphorylation — a chemical and a glycoprotein are converted in a cell to another chemical
  • A nuclear membrane is broken in a cell resulting in an organic molecule
  • A microtubule is moved in a cell from a centrosome toward a cell equator
  • Condense — a diploid chromosome condenses to a condensed diploid chromosome
  • A non-kinetochore microtubule makes contact with another non-kinetochore microtubule in 2 ends
  • Chromosome alignment — a condensed diploid chromosome is aligned in a cell
