nutrient

Protocells (A) In a series of experiments, Jack Szostak and his colleagues mixed fatty acid molecules in water. The molecules formed spherical structures called protocells, with water surrounded by bilayers of fatty acids. (B) A model of a protocell. A portion of the membrane has been cut away to reveal the inside of the protocell and the membrane s bilayer structure. Nutrients and nucleotides pass through the membrane and enter the protocell, where they copy an already present RNA template. The new copies of RNA remain in the protocell.
