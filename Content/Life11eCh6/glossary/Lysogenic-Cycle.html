lysogenic cycle

A type of phage reproductive cycle in which the viral genome becomes incorporated into the bacterial host chromosome as a prophage and does not kill the host.

Definition of a lysogenic cycle

A type of phage reproductive cycle in which the viral genome becomes incorporated into the bacterial host chromosome as a prophage and does not kill the host.

Lysogenic cycle is a type of: phage reproduction cycle .

Participants of a lysogenic cycle

Lysogenic cycle — a temperate phage undergoes the lysogenic cycle resulting in a prophage

Steps of lysogenic cycle

  • A genome is moved by a temperate phage
  • A genome is added to another genome
  • Vegetative reproduction in an organism resulting in another organism
  • Organismal development for a multicellular organism
  • Recombination — 2 genomes and a molecule are recombined resulting in a prophage
