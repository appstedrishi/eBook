dehydration reaction

Definition of a dehydration reaction

A chemical reaction in which two molecules covalently bond to each other with the removal of a water molecule.

Dehydration reaction is a type of: condensation reaction .

Properties of a dehydration reaction

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic reactions
      • positive with respect to an exergonic reaction

Participants of a dehydration reaction

Dehydration reaction consumes 2 molecules and produces a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
