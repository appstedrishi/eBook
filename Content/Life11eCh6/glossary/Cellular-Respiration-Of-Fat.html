Definition of a cellular respiration of fat

Properties of a cellular respiration of fat

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic processes
      • negative with respect to a non spontaneous process

Participants of a cellular respiration of fat

Cellular respiration in an organism using fat resulting in ATP, carbon dioxide and a water molecule. This process releases free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of cellular respiration of fat

  • Oxidative phosphorylation — an ATP synthase converts an ADP, a FADH2 and a phosphate ion in a cell to ATP and a water molecule. This process transforms a proton-motive force to free-energy.
  • Hydrolysis — a lipase converts fat and a water molecule to a chemical and a fatty acid. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • Beta oxidation — a fatty acid is converted in a cell to an acetyl CoA, a FADH2 and an NADH. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
