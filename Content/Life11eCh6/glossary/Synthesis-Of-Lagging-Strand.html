synthesis of lagging strand

The elongation of a discontinuously synthesized DNA strand that elongates by means of Okazaki fragments, each synthesized in a 5' - 3' direction away from the replication fork.

Definition of a synthesis of lagging strand

The elongation of a discontinuously synthesized DNA strand that elongates by means of Okazaki fragments, each synthesized in a 5' - 3' direction away from the replication fork.

Properties of a synthesis of lagging strand

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic reactions
      • positive with respect to an exergonic reaction

Participants of a synthesis of lagging strand

A DNA polymerase (a catalyst) synthesizes a DNA and a water molecule in a cell using 2 monomers in a replication fork. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.

Steps of synthesis of lagging strand

  • RNA priming in a cell using 2 monomers resulting in a DNA, an RNA, an RNA primer and a water molecule in a replication fork. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • Dehydration reaction consumes 2 monomers and produces a DNA, an RNA and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
  • pyrophosphate is broken by a DNA polymerase resulting in a phosphate group. This process releases energy.
  • A DNA piece and an RNA primer are replaced by a DNA polymerase I in a DNA
  • A ribonucleotide is attached by a DNA ligase and a DNA polymerase to a DNA at a 3 prime end and a replication fork. This process requires energy.
