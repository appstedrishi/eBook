Examples of a daughter cell

  • In cell cycle, cell division — a cell is divided using ATP resulting in another cell. Here, the second cell is a daughter cell.
  • In malignant tumor growth, cell division — a cancer cell is divided using ATP resulting in another cancer cell. Here, the second cancer cell is a daughter cell.
  • In M-phase during animal mitotic cycle, cytoplasm undergoes cytokinesis in an animal cell producing another 2 animal cells. Here, another animal cell is a daughter cell.
  • In animal mitotic cycle, M-phase consumes ATP and produces 2 animal cells. Here, one of the animal cells is a daughter cell.
  • In animal life cycle, meiosis — a diploid cell undergoes meiosis using ATP resulting in a gamete in a testis. Here, the gamete is a daughter cell.
  • In animal growth, cell division — an animal cell is divided using ATP resulting in a cell. Here, the cell is a daughter cell.
  • An animal cell is divided by an object using ATP resulting in another animal cell. Here, the second animal cell is a daughter cell.
  • In alternation of generations, meiosis — a diploid cell undergoes meiosis using ATP resulting in a gamete in a sporophyte. Here, the gamete is a daughter cell.
  • In M-phase during mitotic cell cycle, cytoplasm undergoes cytokinesis in a eukaryotic cell producing another 2 eukaryotic cells. Here, another eukaryotic cell is a daughter cell.
  • In mitotic cell cycle, M-phase consumes ATP and produces 2 eukaryotic cells. Here, one of the eukaryotic cells is a daughter cell.
  • In meiosis-II, a eukaryotic cell is a daughter cell in the cytokinesis in another eukaryotic cell producing the first eukaryotic cell
  • In M-phase, cytokinesis — cytoplasm undergoes cytokinesis in a eukaryotic cell producing another eukaryotic cell. Here, the second eukaryotic cell is a daughter cell.
