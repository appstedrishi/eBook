Definition of a mitochondrion

An organelle in eukaryotic cells that serves as the site of cellular respiration; uses oxygen to to break down organic molecules and synthesize ATP.

Mitochondrion is a type of: semiautonomous organelle .

Functions of a mitochondrion

  • Autoreplication of mitochondrion by a mitochondrion
  • pyruvate is converted by a multienzyme complex inside a eukaryotic cell in a eukaryote to an acetyl CoA, carbon dioxide and an NADH at a mitochondrion. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • In cell communication with mitochondrial signal leading to apoptosis, cellular respiration in a eukaryotic cell using glucose and 6 oxygen molecules resulting in 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, an NADH and 6 water molecules in a mitochondrion. This process transforms activation energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • An ADP and a phosphate ion are converted by an ATP synthase in a eukaryotic cell to ATP and a water molecule in a functional region and a mitochondrion. This process transforms a proton-motive force to free-energy.
  • In eukaryotic oxidative phosphorylation, an ADP, an inorganic phosphate and a phosphate ion are converted by an ATP synthase in a eukaryotic cell to ATP and a water molecule in a mitochondrion. This process transforms activation energy, free-energy and a proton-motive force to another free-energy.
  • energy is transformed by a mitochondrion in a eukaryote to another energy

Figure 10.9
Photophosphorylation In the thylakoid membrane, electrons are passed from photosystem II to photosystem I via a series of electron carriers, including plastoquinone (PQ), cytochrome (Cyt), and plastocyanin (PC). From photosystem I the electrons are passed to ferredoxin (Fd) and then to NADP + reductase. This process results in a proton gradient across the membrane, which drives ATP synthesis.
