synthesis of human protein in bacteria

Following transformation of bacteria with the addition of a gene which encodes human protein, the process of prokaryotic transcription and translation produce the human protein. Human insulin for medical treatment is made in this way.

Participants of a synthesis of human protein in bacteria

Synthesis of human protein in bacteria — a functional protein and a water molecule are synthesized in a cell using 2 monomers in a prokaryote. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.

Steps of synthesis of human protein in bacteria

  • Post translational modification — polypeptide is modified resulting in a functional protein
  • Prokaryotic transcription — a transcription factor transcribes a gene to a 3 prime end from a 5 prime end using ATP and 2 monomers resulting in an MRNA and a water molecule in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • Dehydration reaction consumes 2 monomers and produces a functional protein and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
  • Translation — a ribosome and a TRNA convert polypeptide and a water molecule in a cell from an MRNA using an amino acid, a GTP and 2 monomers. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy and thermal energy.
