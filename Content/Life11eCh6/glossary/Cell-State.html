Some functions of different types of cell states

Expand All Collapse All
  • Walled cell
    • In C4 plant, eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a bundle-sheath cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
    • In C4-photosynthesis, transfer of malate to bundle sheath cell — a malate is transferred to a bundle-sheath cell from a mesophyll cell through a plasmodesmata
    • In C4 plant, cellular work in a bundle-sheath cell using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy.
    • In C4 plant, photosynthesis — carbon dioxide and a water molecule are converted in a bundle-sheath cell to an oxygen molecule and sugar. This process transforms free-energy and visible light to chemical energy.
  • Storage cell
    • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a storage cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • Animal cell
    • In animal mitotic cycle, cellular work in an animal cell using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy.
    • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in an adipose cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
