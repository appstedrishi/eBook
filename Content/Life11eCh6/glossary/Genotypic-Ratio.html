Genotypic ratio

genotypic ratio

The proportion of genotypes found in individuals after a cross. For example, consider a cross between two heterozygous individuals for trait A: Aa X Aa The result is a 1:2:1 ratio for the genotypes AA, Aa, and aa respectively. Put another way, if the parents had 100 offspring, 25 would be expected to have the genotype AA, 50 the genotype Aa and 25 the genotype aa.
