Golgi apparatus

Golgi apparatus

An organelle in eukaryotic cells consisting of stacks of flat membranous sacs that modify, store, and route products of the endoplasmic reticulum and synthesize some products, notably noncellulose carbohydrates.

Definition of Golgi apparatus

An organelle in eukaryotic cells consisting of stacks of flat membranous sacs that modify, store, and route products of the endoplasmic reticulum and synthesize some products, notably noncellulose carbohydrates.

Golgi apparatus is a type of: organelle.

Structure of Golgi apparatus

  • Shape
    • hollow
  • Has region
    • trans face

      A trans face is oriented towards a plasma membrane

      A trans face is facing a plasma membrane

    • cis face

      A cis face is oriented towards an endoplasmic reticulum and a nucleus

      A cis face is near an endoplasmic reticulum

      A cis face is facing an endoplasmic reticulum

  • Has part
    • 1 or more cisternas
    • cisterna

      A cisterna is between a cis face and a trans face

    • protein enzyme
    • inherent to all organelles
      • biomembrane

        A biomembrane is between 2 aqueous solutions

      • aqueous solution

        An aqueous solution is inside an inside face
