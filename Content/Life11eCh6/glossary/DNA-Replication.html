Properties of a DNA replication

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic reactions
      • positive with respect to an exergonic reaction

Participants of a DNA replication

A DNA ligase, a DNA polymerase III and primase copy a DNA (a template) in a cell to a 5 prime end from a 3 prime end using 2 monomers resulting in another DNA and a water molecule. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.

Steps of DNA replication

  • Dehydration reaction consumes 2 monomers and produces a DNA and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
  • DNA proofreading — a DNA polymerase proofreads a damaged DNA strand
  • Mismatch repair — a DNA polymerase, protein and a repair enzyme repair a damaged DNA strand in a DNA in a cell
  • Initiation of replication in a cell and a DNA resulting in a DNA piece and a replication bubble in an origin of replication
  • DNA elongation — a DNA polymerase and a DNA polymerase III DNA elongation a DNA strand in a cell using ATP and a dna nucleotide resulting in a lagging strand and a leading strand in a replication fork
  • DNA replication termination in a cell
