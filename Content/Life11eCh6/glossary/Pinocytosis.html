Properties of a pinocytosis

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic processes
      • positive with respect to a spontaneous process
  • Rate
    • The rate of pinocytosis is directly proportional to the rate of cellular respiration.
  • Importance
    • inherent to all cellular works
      • high with respect to an event

Participants of a pinocytosis

liquid is transported by a eukaryotic cell to cytoplasm inside a vesicle from an extracellular side through a plasma membrane using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.

Steps of pinocytosis

  • A cavity and a vesicle are produced in a plasma membrane
  • An increase
  • Consume of chemical energy and free-energy
  • liquid is carried by a vesicle
  • thermal energy is released by an organic molecule
  • liquid is taken in by a eukaryotic cell to cytoplasm from an extracellular side
