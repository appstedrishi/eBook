Definition of an endoplasmic reticulum

An extensive membranous network in eukaryotic cells, continuous with the outer nuclear membrane and composed of ribosome-studded (rough) and ribosome-free (smooth) regions.

Functions of an endoplasmic reticulum

  • Synthesis of lipid — lipid is synthesized in a cell using a chemical in an endoplasmic reticulum. This process requires free-energy.

Some functions of different types of endoplasmic reticulum

Expand All Collapse All
  • Smooth endoplasmic reticulum
    • A calcium ion is stored by a smooth endoplasmic reticulum
    • Metabolism in an organism using a carbohydrate in a smooth endoplasmic reticulum
    • In liver cell, alcohol, a barbiturate and a drug are detoxified by a liver cell using 2 protein enzymes in a smooth endoplasmic reticulum
  • Rough endoplasmic reticulum
    • Metabolism in an organism in a rough endoplasmic reticulum
    • Synthesis of membrane protein — membrane protein and a water molecule are synthesized in a eukaryotic cell using 2 polypeptides in a rough endoplasmic reticulum. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
    • A hydrolase is transferred from a rough endoplasmic reticulum to Golgi apparatus
