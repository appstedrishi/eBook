Functions of a concentration gradient

  • Diffusion of an anion and a hydrophilic compound by means of a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution from cytoplasm with a transport protein from a hypertonic solution through a plasma membrane using a membrane potential to an extra cellular matrix. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
  • Diffusion of a cation and a hydrophilic compound by means of a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a transport protein from a hypertonic solution through a plasma membrane using a membrane potential. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
  • In chloroplast, release of a hydrogen ion by a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a proton pump from a hypertonic solution through a thylakoid membrane using a membrane potential. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
