Chloroplasts Feed the World The electron micrographs show chloroplasts from a leaf of corn. Chloroplasts are large compared with mitochondria and contain extensive networks of thylakoid membranes. These membranes contain the green pigment chlorophyll, and are the sites where light energy is converted into chemical energy for the synthesis of carbohydrates from CO 2 and H 2 O.
