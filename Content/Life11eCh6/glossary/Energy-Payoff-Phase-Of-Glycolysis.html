Participants of an energy payoff phase of glycolysis

Energy payoff phase of glycolysis in a cell using PGAP, 4 ADPs, 2 glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate and NAD plus resulting in 4 ATP, 2 hydrogen ions, 2 NADHs, 2 pyruvate and 2 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Steps of energy payoff phase of glycolysis

  1. Chemical reaction — glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate is converted to PGAP in an active site
  2. Substrate level phosphorylation — PGAP is converted in a cell to ATP and a 3 phosphoglycerate. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  3. Isomerization reaction consumes a 3 phosphoglycerate and produces a 2-phosphoglycerate
  4. Dehydration reaction consumes a molecule and a 2-phosphoglycerate and produces phosphoenolpyruvate and a water molecule. This process requires free-energy.
  5. Substrate level phosphorylation — an ADP and phosphoenolpyruvate are converted in a cell to ATP and pyruvate. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
