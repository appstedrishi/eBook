Definition of a miller urey experiment

Experimental closed system created by Stanley Miller and Harold Urey, used to mimic the conditions present on early Earth. The Miller-Urey experiment provided evidence in support of the theory of abiotic synthesis of organic materials important to life.

Participants of a miller urey experiment

Miller urey experiment using an ammonia gas, a hydrogen molecule, a methane and a water vapour resulting in an amino acid, a formaldehyde, a hydrocarbon molecule, a hydrogen cyanide and an organic molecule substance in a laboratory. This process requires an electric current pulse.

Steps of miller urey experiment

  1. Boiling in water resulting in a water vapour
  2. A water vapour is flowed to a flask from another flask
  3. An electric current pulse is added to a homogeneous mixture
  4. Chemical reaction consumes ammonia, a hydrogen molecule, a methane and a water molecule and produces an amino acid, a formaldehyde, a hydrocarbon molecule and a hydrogen cyanide
  5. An aqueous solution is flowed to a flask from another flask
  6. Cool in an aqueous solution
  7. Condense produces an aqueous solution
  8. An aqueous solution is flowed to a flask from another flask
  9. Analysis in an aqueous solution
  10. An aqueous solution is flowed to a flask from another flask
