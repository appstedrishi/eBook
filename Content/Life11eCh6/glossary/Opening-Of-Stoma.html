Steps of opening of stoma

  • Active transport — a proton pump transports a hydrogen ion in a cell and a concentration gradient to a solution from a hypotonic solution through a biomembrane using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • A potassium ion is moved by a membrane potential in a plant organ from an epidermal cell
  • A potassium ion is moved in a guard cell to a central vacuole from cytoplasm through a tonoplast
  • Facilitated diffusion of water using aquaporin — a concentration gradient and a water potential gradient diffuse an oxygen molecule, water and a water molecule to a hypertonic solution with a channel protein from a hypotonic solution through a plasma membrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
  • Increase produces a turgid cell
  • Change of state — a stoma is changed state resulting in another stoma
  • An anion is moved in a plant organ to a biological region from a region resulting in a turgid cell

Figure 6.11 A Gated Channel Protein Opens in Response to a Stimulus
The channel protein has a pore of polar amino acids and water. It is anchored in the hydrophobic bilayer by its outer coating of nonpolar R groups. The protein changes its three-dimensional shape when a stimulus molecule (ligand) binds to it, opening the pore so that specific hydrophilic substances can pass through. Other channels open in response to an electric potential (voltage) or mechanical stimuli.
