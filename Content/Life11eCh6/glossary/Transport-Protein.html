Functions of a transport protein

  • Facilitated diffusion — a chemical diffuses to an aqueous solution using a transport protein from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
  • A transport protein repels from a hydrophobic region
  • Release of a hydrogen ion by an ATP synthase, a concentration gradient and a proton pump in a cell and another concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a transport protein from a hypertonic solution through a plasma membrane using a membrane potential. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
