A type of eukaryotic cell only found in animals.

Animal cell is a type of: cell without cell-wall and eukaryotic cell.

Communicating Junctions (A) An animal cell may contain hundreds of gap junctions connecting it to neighboring cells. The pores of gap junctions allow small molecules to pass from cell to cell, ensuring similar concentrations of important signaling molecules in adjacent cells so that the cells can coordinate their activities. Functions of an animal cell

  • A hydrogen ion is transported by a carrier protein to a solution from an aqueous solution through a biomembrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in an animal cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • In maintenance of concentration of calcium ion In animal cell, a calcium ion is absorbed by 2 carrier proteins in an animal cell to an aqueous solution from cytosol through an ER membrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
