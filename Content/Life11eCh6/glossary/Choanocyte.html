choanocyte

Definition of a choanocyte

A flagellated feeding cell found in sponges. Also called a collar cell, it has a collar-like ring that traps food particles around the base of its flagellum.

Choanocyte is a type of: invertebrate cell .

Functions of a choanocyte

  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a choanocyte to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
  • An object is transported by a choanocyte to cytoplasm inside a food vacuole from an extracellular side through an animal plasma membrane using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
