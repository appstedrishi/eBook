Participants of an aquatic nitrogen cycle

Aquatic nitrogen cycle — a nitrogen molecule undergoes the aquatic nitrogen cycle in a deep body of water in an aquatic ecosystem

Steps of aquatic nitrogen cycle

  • A solution is taken in by a plankton to a cell from a deep body of water
  • Nitrogen fixation — a nitrogen fixing bacterium fixes in a deep body of water using a nitrogen molecule resulting in ammonia
  • Ammonification — detritus undergoes ammonification by an ammonifying bacterium, fungus and an organism using an organic molecule resulting in ammonia and another organic molecule
  • A nitrogen fixing bacterium is consumed by an organism
  • Death — an organism is deathed resulting in a dead organism
  • Decomposition — fungus, an organism and a prokaryote decompose a dead organism resulting in an inorganic molecule and an organic molecule
  • Nitrification — a nitrifying bacterium nitrifications using a chemical resulting in another chemical
  • Chemical reaction consumes ammonia and a hydrogen ion and produces an ammonium ion
  • Decomposition — fungus, an organism and a prokaryote decompose detritus resulting in an amino acid and an inorganic molecule
