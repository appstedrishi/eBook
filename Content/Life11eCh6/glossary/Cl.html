Formation of Sodium and Chloride Ions When a sodium atom reacts with a chlorine atom, the more electronegative chlorine fills its outermost shell by stealing an electron from the sodium. In so doing, the chlorine atom becomes a negatively charged chloride ion (Cl ). With one less electron, the sodium atom becomes a positively charged sodium ion (Na + ).
