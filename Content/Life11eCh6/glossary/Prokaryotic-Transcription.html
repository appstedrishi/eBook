Prokaryotic transcription

prokaryotic transcription

The process where mRNA transcripts of the prokaryotic DNA are produced, to be translated into proteins. Prokaryotic transcription simultaneously with translation in the cytoplasm, unlike in eukaryotes where the process is separated due to the presence of the nuclear envelope.

Participants of a prokaryotic transcription

A gene is transcribed by a transcription factor to a 3 prime end from a 5 prime end using 2 ATP and a monomer resulting in an MRNA and a water molecule in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
