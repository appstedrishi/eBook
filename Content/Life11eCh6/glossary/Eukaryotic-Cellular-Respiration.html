Definition of a eukaryotic cellular respiration

The catabolic pathways of aerobic respiration, which break down organic molecules for the production of ATP. A total of 30 ATP are produced in this process. The step of pyruvate oxidation takes place in mitochondria.

Participants of a eukaryotic cellular respiration

Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of eukaryotic cellular respiration

  1. Glycolysis — 2 ADPs, glucose and 2 NAD plus are converted in a eukaryotic cell to 2 ATP, carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 2 pyruvate in an active site and cytosol. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to free-energy.
  2. Pyruvate oxidation — a multienzyme complex converts pyruvate in a cell to an acetyl CoA, carbon dioxide and 2 NADHs at cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to free-energy.
  3. Citric acid cycle — an acetyl CoA, 3 NAD plus and oxaloacetate are converted in a cell to ATP, 2 carbon dioxide, a FADH2 and 3 NADHs. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to free-energy.
  4. Eukaryotic oxidative phosphorylation — an ATP synthase converts an ADP, an NADH, an oxygen molecule and a phosphate ion in a eukaryotic cell to ATP, another NADH and a water molecule in an active site and cytoplasm. This process transforms a proton-motive force to free-energy.
