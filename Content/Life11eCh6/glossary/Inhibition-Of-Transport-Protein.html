Inhibition of transport protein

inhibition of transport protein

The transport proteins can be inhibited by molecules that resemble the normal "substrate." This occurs when the imposter competes with the normally transported solute by binding to the transport protein.

Definition of an inhibition of transport protein

The transport proteins can be inhibited by molecules that resemble the normal "substrate." This occurs when the imposter competes with the normally transported solute by binding to the transport protein.

Inhibition of transport protein is a type of: inhibition.
