Properties of a cellular respiration of sucrose

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a cellular respiration of sucrose

NAD plus is converted by an organism to ATP, carbon dioxide, pyruvate, sucrose and a water molecule. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of cellular respiration of sucrose

  1. Hydrolysis
  2. Glycolysis
  3. Pyruvate oxidation
  4. Citric acid cycle
  5. Oxidative phosphorylation
