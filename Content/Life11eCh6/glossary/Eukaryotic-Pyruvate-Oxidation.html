Participants of a eukaryotic pyruvate oxidation

pyruvate is converted by a multienzyme complex inside a eukaryotic cell in a eukaryote to an acetyl CoA, carbon dioxide and an NADH at a mitochondrion. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Steps of eukaryotic pyruvate oxidation

  1. Decarboxylation consumes pyruvate and produces carbon dioxide and an organic molecule. This process requires energy.
