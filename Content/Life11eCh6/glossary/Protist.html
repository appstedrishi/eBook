Definition of a protist

An informal term applied to any eukaryote that is not a plant, animal, or fungus. Most protists are unicellular, though some are colonial or multicellular.

The Tree of Life The earliest division of life was between the Bacteria and Archaea. Later, one lineage of Archaea incorporated specializations (including a cell nucleus) that allowed the repeated evolution of multicellularity; we call this specialized group Eukarya. The aqua branches within Eukarya represent various groups of microbial eukaryotes, more commonly known as protists. The organisms on any one branch share a common ancestor.
