An acetyl CoA also does…

  • Citric acid cycle In eukaryote — a eukaryote undergoes citric acid cycle in a eukaryotic cell using an acetyl CoA, activation energy, NAD plus, oxaloacetate and potential energy resulting in ATP, carbon dioxide, a FADH2, free-energy and an NADH in a mitochondrial matrix. Here, the acetyl CoA is a reactant.
  • In eukaryotic cellular respiration, citric acid cycle — an acetyl CoA, activation energy, NAD plus, oxaloacetate and potential energy are converted in a cell to ATP, carbon dioxide, a FADH2, free-energy and 2 NADHs. Here, the acetyl CoA is a reactant.
  • Oxidation — an electron transfers from an acetyl CoA to oxaloacetate. Here, the acetyl CoA is an electron donor.
  • In cellular respiration of glycogen, citric acid cycle — an acetyl CoA, activation energy, NAD plus and oxaloacetate are converted in a cell to ATP, carbon dioxide, a FADH2, free-energy and an NADH. Here, the acetyl CoA is a reactant.
  • In cellular respiration, citric acid cycle — an acetyl CoA, activation energy, NAD plus and oxaloacetate are converted in a cell to ATP, carbon dioxide, a FADH2, free-energy and an NADH. Here, the acetyl CoA is a reactant.
