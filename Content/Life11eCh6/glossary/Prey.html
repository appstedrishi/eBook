Figure 1.11
Energy Can Be Used Immediately or Stored (A) Animal cells break down food molecules and use the energy contained in the chemical bonds of those molecules to do mechanical work, such as running, flying, and jumping. This image is a European roller flying with mammal prey in its bill. (B) The cells of this Arctic ground squirrel have broken down the complex carbohydrates in the plants it consumed and converted those molecules into fats. The fats are stored in the animal s body to provide an energy supply for the cold months.
