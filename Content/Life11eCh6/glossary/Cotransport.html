Definition of a cotransport

The coupling of the "downhill" diffusion of one substance to the "uphill" transport of another against its own concentration gradient.

Participants of a cotransport

A chemical, free-energy and a hydrophilic compound are transported by a cotransporter through a biomembrane

Steps of cotransport

  • Active transport using ATP — a chemical and a hydrophilic compound are transported by means of a cotransporter to an aqueous solution from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • Facilitated diffusion using carrier protein — a chemical and a hydrophilic compound diffuse by means of a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a cotransporter from a hypertonic solution through a biomembrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
