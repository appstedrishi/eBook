Functions of a zygote

  • 2 hydrogen ions are transported by an ATP synthase and a carrier protein to a hypotonic solution from a hypertonic solution through an inner membrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a zygote to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

A zygote also does…

  • In embryogenesis, M-phase consumes ATP and produces 2 eukaryotic cells. Here, a zygote is a parent cell.
  • In embryogenesis, cell division — a zygote is divided using ATP resulting in a cell. Here, the zygote is a parent cell.
