Prokaryotic ribosome

prokaryotic ribosome

A cell structure in prokaryotes that receives the mRNA and translates the genetic information into proteins during protein synthesis. They are composed of three types of rRNA, the 16S rRNA, 23S rRNA, and 5S rRNA which combine with proteins to form a complete, functional ribosome.

Definition of a prokaryotic ribosome

A cell structure in prokaryotes that receives the mRNA and translates the genetic information into proteins during protein synthesis. They are composed of three types of rRNA, the 16S rRNA, 23S rRNA, and 5S rRNA which combine with proteins to form a complete, functional ribosome.
