Functions of a plant cell

  • 2 hydrogen ions are transported by an ATP synthase and a carrier protein to a hypotonic solution from a hypertonic solution through a plasma membrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a plant cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.

A plant cell also does…

  • An organic molecule is secreted by a plant cell to an extracellular side with a transport vesicle from cytoplasm through a plasma membrane using chemical energy, free-energy and another organic molecule. Here, the plant cell is a secretor.
