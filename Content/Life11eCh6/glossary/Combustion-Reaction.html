combustion reaction

In combustion reactions, a hydrocarbon reacts with oxygen to form carbon dioxide and water.

Definition of a combustion reaction

In combustion reactions, a hydrocarbon reacts with oxygen to form carbon dioxide and water.

Combustion reaction is a type of: redox reaction and exergonic reaction .

Properties of a combustion reaction

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a combustion reaction

Combustion reaction consumes 2 chemicals and produces another chemical. This process releases free-energy.

Steps of combustion reaction

  • Reduction
  • Oxidation

Types of combustion reactions

  • combustion of fossil fuel
  • chemical reaction between methane and oxygen
