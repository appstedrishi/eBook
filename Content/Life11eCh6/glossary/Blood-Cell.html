Definition of a blood cell

A blood cell, also called a hematocyte, is a cell of any type normally found in blood. In mammals, blood cells fall into three categories - Red blood cell, white blood cell, platelets.

Functions of a blood cell

  • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in an erythrocyte to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
