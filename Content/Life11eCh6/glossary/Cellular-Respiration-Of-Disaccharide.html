Definition of a cellular respiration of disaccharide

Properties of a cellular respiration of disaccharide

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a cellular respiration of disaccharide

A disaccharide is converted by an organism to ATP, carbon dioxide and a water molecule. This process releases free-energy and thermal energy.

Steps of cellular respiration of disaccharide

  1. Hydrolysis
  2. Glycolysis
  3. Pyruvate oxidation
  4. Citric acid cycle
  5. Oxidative phosphorylation

Types of cellular respiration of disaccharides

  • cellular respiration of sucrose
