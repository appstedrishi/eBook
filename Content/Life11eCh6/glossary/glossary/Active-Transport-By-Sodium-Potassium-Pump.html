Participants of an active transport by sodium potassium pump

A potassium ion and a sodium ion are transported by a cell by an electrochemical gradient and a sodium-potassium pump through a biomembrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy.

Steps of active transport by sodium potassium pump

  • Active transport using ATP — a sodium ion is transported by means of a sodium-potassium pump to an aqueous solution from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • Active movement of ions — a sodium-potassium pump transports a potassium ion to a solution from an aqueous solution through a biomembrane using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
