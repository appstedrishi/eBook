Properties of an active movement of ions

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all endergonic processes
      • positive with respect to a spontaneous process
  • Rate
    • The rate of active movement of ions is directly proportional to the rate of cellular respiration.
  • Importance
    • inherent to all cellular works
      • high with respect to an event

Participants of an active movement of ions

An ion is transported by a carrier protein to a solution from another solution through a biomembrane using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.

Steps of active movement of ions

  • Consume of chemical energy
  • thermal energy is released by an organic molecule
  • An ion is moved by a carrier protein through a biomembrane to a solution from another solution through a hydrophobic core

Types of active movement of ionses

  • uptake of calcium ion by mitochondria
  • uptake of calcium ion by endoplasmic reticulum
  • uptake of calcium ion by chloroplast
  • release of calcium ion by cell
  • movement of hydrogen ion to mitochondrial matrix
  • generation of hydrogen ion gradient across biomembrane
  • active transport by proton pump
