Inducible operon

inducible operon

A functioning unit of genomic material containing a cluster of genes under the control of a single regulatory signal or promoter. The operon is usually turned off but can be stimulated when a specific, small molecule interacts with a regulatory protein.

Definition of an inducible operon

A functioning unit of genomic material containing a cluster of genes under the control of a single regulatory signal or promoter. The operon is usually turned off but can be stimulated when a specific, small molecule interacts with a regulatory protein.
