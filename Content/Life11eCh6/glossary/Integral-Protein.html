Integral protein

integral protein

A membrane protein with a hydrophobic region that extends into and sometimes completely spans the hydrophobic interior of the membrane. It also contains at least one hydrophilic region that is in contact with the aqueous solution at the surface of the membrane or lining any channels it may have.

Definition of an integral protein

A membrane protein with a hydrophobic region that extends into and sometimes completely spans the hydrophobic interior of the membrane. It also contains at least one hydrophilic region that is in contact with the aqueous solution at the surface of the membrane or lining any channels it may have.

Integral protein is a type of: amphipathic molecule and membrane protein.
