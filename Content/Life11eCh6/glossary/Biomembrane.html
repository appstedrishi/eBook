Definition of a biomembrane

A biological membrane or biomembrane is an enclosing or separating membrane that acts as a selective barrier, within or around a cell.

A Gated Ion Channel The acetylcholine receptor (AChR) is a ligand-gated ion channel for sodium ions. It is made up of five polypeptide subunits. Some functions of different types of biomembranes

  • Plasma membrane
    • Transport of an organic molecule in a cell to an extracellular side with a transport vesicle from cytoplasm through a plasma membrane using another organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
    • A cell is recognized by another cell at a plasma membrane
    • In receptor mediated endocytosis, directed motion — a chemical is moved in a plasma membrane through a coated pit, an extracellular side, a hydrophobic core, an inside face and an intermembrane space
    • In coated pit, transport of a chemical by means of a cell surface receptor in a eukaryotic cell to cytoplasm inside a coated vesicle from an extracellular side using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.
  • Outer membrane
    • In cell communication with mitochondrial signal leading to apoptosis, a semiautonomous organelle is confined by an outer membrane
