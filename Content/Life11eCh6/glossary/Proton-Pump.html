Proton pump

proton pump

An active transport protein in a cell membrane that uses ATP to transport hydrogen ions out of a cell against their concentration gradient, generating a membrane potential in the process.

Definition of a proton pump

An active transport protein in a cell membrane that uses ATP to transport hydrogen ions out of a cell against their concentration gradient, generating a membrane potential in the process.

Proton pump is a type of: carrier protein and electrogenic pump .

Functions of a proton pump

  • Facilitated diffusion using carrier protein — a chemical diffuses by means of a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a proton pump from a hypertonic solution through a biomembrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
  • A hydrogen ion gradient and a membrane potential are maintained by a proton pump
  • Active transport by proton pump — a proton pump actives transport by proton pump a hydrogen ion in a cell and a concentration gradient to an aqueous solution from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane using ATP resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • A proton pump repels from a hydrophobic region
  • A membrane potential is created by a proton pump
