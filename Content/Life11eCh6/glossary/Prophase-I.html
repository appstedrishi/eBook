Prophase-I

prophase-I

The first phase of meiosis where the chromosomes begin to condense and become organized for the rest of the meiotic process. Crossing over also occurs during prophase I.

Definition of a prophase-I

The first phase of meiosis where the chromosomes begin to condense and become organized for the rest of the meiotic process. Crossing over also occurs during prophase I.

Prophase-I is a type of: meiotic event.

Participants of a prophase-I

Prophase-I — a diploid cell undergoes prophase-I in a cell

Steps of prophase-I

  • A create
  • Junction in a cell through a homologous chromosome resulting in a synaptonemal complex
  • Synapsis in a cell resulting in a tetrad
  • Create produces a tetrad
  • A microtubule is attached to a kinetochore
  • A mitotic spindle is produced in a centrosome
  • Growth of a microtubule
  • Move — a microtubule moves a chromosome in a cell to a metaphase plate using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy.
  • Move apart of a centrosome
  • Destroy of a nuclear membrane and a nucleolus
