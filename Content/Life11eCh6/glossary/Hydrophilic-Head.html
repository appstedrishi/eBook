hydrophilic head

The "head" part of a molecule that is polar (having a partial charge due to electronegativity) and therefore does not resist interaction with water.

Definition of a hydrophilic head

The "head" part of a molecule that is polar (having a partial charge due to electronegativity) and therefore does not resist interaction with water.

Hydrophilic head is a type of: hydrophilic end.

Phospholipids (A) Phosphatidylcholine (lecithin) demonstrates the structure of a phospholipid molecule. In other phospholipids, the amino acid serine, the sugar alcohol inositol, or other compounds replace choline. (B) In an aqueous environment, hydrophobic interactions bring the tails of phospholipids together in the interior of a bilayer. The hydrophilic heads face outward on both sides of the bilayer, where they interact with the surrounding water molecules.
