Definition of an endomembranous system

The collection of membranes inside and surrounding a eukaryotic cell, related either through direct physical contact or by the transfer of membranous vesicles; includes the plasma membrane, the nuclear envelope, the smooth and rough endoplasmic reticulum, the Golgi apparatus, lysosomes, vesicles, and vacuoles.

Endomembranous system is a type of: cellular region and aggregate.

Structure of an endomembranous system

  • Has part
    • rough endoplasmic reticulum

      A rough endoplasmic reticulum is inside cytosol

    • transport vesicle
    • nuclear membrane

      A nuclear membrane is between 2 aqueous solutions

      A nuclear membrane is along a nuclear lamina

      A nuclear membrane is next to an ER membrane

    • lysosome

      A lysosome is inside cytosol

    • Golgi apparatus

      Golgi apparatus is inside cytosol

    • plasma membrane

      A plasma membrane is between 2 aqueous solutions

      A plasma membrane is next to a nuclear membrane

    • smooth endoplasmic reticulum

      A smooth endoplasmic reticulum is inside cytosol

      A smooth endoplasmic reticulum is next to a rough endoplasmic reticulum

    • vacuole

Functions of an endomembranous system

  • A plasma membrane is synthesized by an endomembranous system in a eukaryotic cell. This process requires free-energy.
  • A plasma membrane is moved by an endomembranous system in a eukaryotic cell
