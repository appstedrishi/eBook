Definition of a cell

The smallest unit of life; a living body contained in a membrane with genetic material and the ability to exchange energy.

Functions of a cell

  • Cellular work in a cell using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy.

Some functions of different types of cells

Expand All Collapse All
  • Unicellular organism
    • detritus is denitrified by a denitrifying bacterium and fungus resulting in an inorganic molecule and an organic molecule
    • In mammal, a vitamin is produced by a bacterium using an organic molecule
    • Ammonification — detritus undergoes ammonification by an ammonifying bacterium and fungus using a monomer resulting in ammonia and an organic molecule
  • Cell state
    • In animal mitotic cycle, cellular work in an animal cell using an organic molecule. This process requires chemical energy.
    • In adipose tissue, eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, 2 NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in an adipose cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
    • alcohol, a barbiturate and a drug are detoxified by a liver cell using a protein enzyme
    • glycogen is stored by a muscle cell
    • pepsinogen and thermal energy are released by a chief cell to a solution from an aqueous solution
    • Defense by cnidocyte
    • Immune response — an immune cell responds within an immune system
    • In C4-photosynthesis, transfer of malate to bundle sheath cell — a malate is transferred to a bundle-sheath cell from a mesophyll cell through a plasmodesmata
    • A bacterium is recognized by a macrophage
    • In C4 plant, photosynthesis — carbon dioxide and a water molecule are converted in a bundle-sheath cell to an oxygen molecule and sugar. This process transforms free-energy and visible light to chemical energy.
    • A bacterium is destroyed by a macrophage in a lysosome
    • In food processing In Cnidaria, a digestive enzyme is secreted by a gastrodermal cell
  • Eukaryotic cell
    • Eukaryotic cellular respiration — glucose, NAD plus and 6 oxygen molecules are converted in a eukaryotic cell to 32 ATP, 6 carbon dioxide, 2 NADHs and 6 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy and potential energy to chemical energy, free-energy and thermal energy.
    • 2 hydrogen ions are transported by an ATP synthase and a carrier protein to a hypotonic solution from a hypertonic solution through an inner membrane using an organic molecule resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
    • A calcium ion is absorbed by 2 carrier proteins in a eukaryotic cell to an aqueous solution from cytosol through an ER membrane using ATP. This process requires chemical energy and free-energy.

A cell also does…

  • In cell cycle, cell division — a cell is divided using ATP resulting in another cell. Here, the second cell is a daughter cell.
  • In animal growth, cell division — an animal cell is divided using ATP resulting in a cell. Here, the cell is a daughter cell.
  • DNA transfer — a DNA piece and a proto-oncogene are transferred to a cell to a prokaryote resulting in cancer, an oncogene and recombinants. Here, another cell is a host.
  • A cell is a host in the replication of a genome
  • A cell is infected by an infectious particle. Here, the cell is a host.
  • A cell is infected by an HIV. Here, the cell is a host.
  • In signal generation during communication through cell junction, secretion — a cell secretes a chemical. Here, the cell is a secretor.
  • In cell cycle, cell division — a cell is divided using ATP resulting in another cell. Here, the first cell is a parent cell.
