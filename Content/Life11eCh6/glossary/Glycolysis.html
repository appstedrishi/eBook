Definition of a glycolysis

The splitting of glucose into pyruvate. Glycolysis occurs in almost all living cells, serving as the starting point for fermentation or cellular respiration.

Glycolysis is a type of: catabolic pathway .

Properties of a glycolysis

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a glycolysis

Glycolysis — 2 ADPs, glucose, glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate and 2 NAD plus are converted in a cell to 2 ATP, 2 NADHs and 2 pyruvate in an active site and cytosol. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Steps of glycolysis

  • Energy investment phase of glycolysis in a cell using 2 ATP and glucose resulting in 2 ADPs and 2 glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • Energy payoff phase of glycolysis in a cell using 4 ADPs, 2 glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate and NAD plus resulting in 4 ATP, 2 hydrogen ions, 2 NADHs, 2 pyruvate and 2 water molecules. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • Oxidation — an electron transfers from glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate to NAD plus. This process requires activation energy.
  • Reduction — an electron transfers from glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate to NAD plus. This process requires activation energy.
