Definition of a hydrolysis

A chemical reaction that breaks bonds between two molecules by the addition of water; often functions in disassembly of polymers to monomers.

Hydrolysis is a type of: decomposition reaction and exergonic reaction .

Properties of a hydrolysis

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a hydrolysis

Hydrolysis consumes a chemical and a water molecule and produces another 2 chemicals. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Types of hydrolyses

  • inactivation of cAMP
  • hydrolysis of sucrose
  • dephosphorylation of ATP
