Properties of a lactic acid fermentation

  • Free energy change
    • inherent to all exergonic reactions
      • negative with respect to an endergonic reaction

Participants of a lactic acid fermentation

Lactic acid fermentation — glucose and NAD plus are converted in a cell to lactate and at least 2 organic molecules (2 ATP, an NADH, pyruvate) in cytoplasm. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.

Steps of lactic acid fermentation

  • Glycolysis — 2 ADPs, glucose and 2 NAD plus are converted in a cell to 2 ATP, lactate, 2 NADHs, 2 pyruvate and a water molecule in cytosol. This process transforms activation energy to free-energy.
  • Redox reaction consumes an NADH and pyruvate and produces lactate and NAD plus

Types of lactic acid fermentations

  • lactic acid fermentation In muscle cell
  • lactic acid fermentation by fungi
  • lactic acid fermentation by bacteria

Fermentation Glycolysis produces pyruvate, ATP, and NADH from glucose. Lactic acid fermentation uses NADH as a reducing agent to reduce pyruvate to lactate, thus regenerating NAD + to keep glycolysis operating. In alcoholic fermentation, pyruvate from glycolysis is converted into acetaldehyde, and CO 2 is released. NADH from glycolysis is used to reduce acetaldehyde to ethanol, thus regenerating NAD + to keep glycolysis operating.
