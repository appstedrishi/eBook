Photorespiration

photorespiration

A metabolic pathway that consumes oxygen and ATP, releases carbon dioxide, and decreases photosynthetic output. Photorespiration generally occurs on hot, dry, bright days, when stomata close and the oxygen concentration in the leaf exceeds that of carbon dioxide.

Definition of a photorespiration

A metabolic pathway that consumes oxygen and ATP, releases carbon dioxide, and decreases photosynthetic output. Photorespiration generally occurs on hot, dry, bright days, when stomata close and the oxygen concentration in the leaf exceeds that of carbon dioxide.

Participants of a photorespiration

Photorespiration — ATP, an oxygen molecule and ribulose bisphosphate are converted in a plant cell to a chemical in an active site. This process transforms activation energy and visible light to free-energy.

Steps of photorespiration

  • Synthesis reaction — an organic molecule and a 3 phosphoglycerate are synthesized from an oxygen molecule and ribulose bisphosphate at a stroma
  • Dephosphorylation — an organic molecule is converted to an anion and another 2 organic molecules in a stroma. This process requires energy.
  • Movement of carbon dioxide to an object outside a mitochondrion out of a mitochondrial matrix through a space
  • An organic molecule is moved to a mitochondrion from a stroma
  • Chemical reaction — an organic molecule is converted to carbon dioxide and a serine in a mitochondrion
  • Chemical reaction — an organic molecule is converted to carbon dioxide and a serine in a peroxisome
  • carbon dioxide is moved to atmosphere out of an aqueous solution through a biological region
  • An organic molecule is moved to a peroxisome from a stroma
