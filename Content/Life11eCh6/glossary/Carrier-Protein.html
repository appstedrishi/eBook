Carrier protein

carrier protein

Carrier proteins are proteins involved in the movement of ions, small molecules, or macromolecules, such as another protein, across a biological membrane.

Definition of a carrier protein

Carrier proteins are proteins involved in the movement of ions, small molecules, or macromolecules, such as another protein, across a biological membrane.

Carrier protein is a type of: transport protein .

Figure 6.12 A Carrier Protein Facilitates Diffusion
The glucose transporter is a carrier protein that allows glucose to enter the cell at a faster rate than would be possible by simple diffusion. (A) The transporter binds to glucose, brings it into the membrane interior, then changes shape, releasing glucose into the cell cytoplasm. (B) The graph shows the rate of glucose entry via a carrier versus the concentration of glucose outside the cell. As the glucose concentration increases, the rate of diffusion increases until the point at which all the available transporters are being used (the system is saturated).

Functions of a carrier protein

  • Facilitated diffusion using carrier protein — a chemical diffuses by means of a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a carrier protein from a hypertonic solution through a biomembrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.

Some functions of different types of carrier proteins

Expand All Collapse All
  • Proton pump
    • A hydrogen ion gradient is maintained by a proton pump
    • Active transport by proton pump — a proton pump actives transport by proton pump a hydrogen ion in a cell and a concentration gradient to an aqueous solution from another aqueous solution through a biomembrane using ATP resulting in a hydrogen ion gradient. This process transforms chemical energy and free-energy to a proton-motive force.
  • Cotransporter
    • 2 chemicals and free-energy are transported by a cotransporter through a biomembrane
    • Release of a chemical by a concentration gradient in a cell and another concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a cotransporter from a hypertonic solution through a biomembrane. This process transforms potential energy to free-energy.
