Metaphase

metaphase

The third stage of mitosis, in which the spindle is complete and the chromosomes, attached to microtubules at their kinetochores, are all aligned at the metaphase plate.

Definition of a metaphase

The third stage of mitosis, in which the spindle is complete and the chromosomes, attached to microtubules at their kinetochores, are all aligned at the metaphase plate.

Metaphase is a type of: mitotic stage.

Participants of a metaphase

Metaphase in a cell resulting in another cell

Steps of metaphase

  • Microtubule elongation of a non-kinetochore microtubule
  • Microtubule elongation of a non-kinetochore microtubule
  • A non-kinetochore microtubule makes contact with another non-kinetochore microtubule
  • 2 diploid chromosomes are joined at a cell equator

The Human Karyotype (A) Chromosomes from a human cell in metaphase. The DNA of each chromosome pair has a specific nucleotide sequence that is stained by a particular colored dye, so that the chromosomes in a homologous pair share a distinctive color. Each chromosome at this stage is composed of two chromatids, but they cannot be distinguished. At the upper right is an interphase nucleus. (B) This karyogram, produced by computerized analysis of the image on the left, shows homologous pairs lined up together and numbered, clearly revealing the individual s karyotype.

Chromatid Attachment and Separation The cohesin protein complex holds sister chromatids together at the centromere. The enzyme separase hydrolyzes cohesin at the end of metaphase, allowing the chromatids to separate into daughter chromosomes.
