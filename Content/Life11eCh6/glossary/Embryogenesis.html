Steps of embryogenesis

  • Cellular response — a cell responds using protein
  • Cell differentiation induction — a chemical induces cell differentiation in a eukaryotic cell
  • An organ development
  • Regulation — a concentration gradient regulates an anatomical structure in an embryo
  • Create produces a concentration gradient
  • Synthesis of protein — 2 monomers are converted in a cell to protein and a water molecule. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • A pattern formation
  • Two cell stage produces a two cell embryo
  • Eight cell stage produces an eight cell embryo
  • Cell differentiation for tissue formation — a eukaryotic cell is differentiated resulting in a differentiated cell
  • Cell division — a cell is divided using ATP resulting in 2 cells (2 embryonic cells)
  • Differentiation in an embryonic cell resulting in a differentiated cell
  • Cell division — a cell is divided using ATP resulting in 2 cells
  • Cell division — a zygote is divided using ATP resulting in 2 cells
  • Cell differentiation — a eukaryotic cell is differentiated resulting in a differentiated cell
  • Cell differentiation — a eukaryotic cell is differentiated resulting in a differentiated cell
  • M-phase consumes ATP and produces 2 cells (2 eukaryotic cells)
  • A morphogenesis
  • Create produces tissue
