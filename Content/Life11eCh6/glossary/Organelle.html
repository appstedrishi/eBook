Some functions of different types of organelles

Expand All Collapse All
  • Semiautonomous organelle
    • Autoreplication of semiautonomous organelle by a semiautonomous organelle
  • Nucleus
    • Synthesis of an RNA and a water molecule by a transcription factor in a cell and a transcription unit toward a 3 prime end in another cell using a gene from a 5 prime end using ATP and a monomer in a nucleus. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
  • Peroxisome
    • In animate thing, alcohol and 2 drugs are detoxified by a liver cell using a protein enzyme in a peroxisome
  • Vacuole
    • An organic molecule is stored by a central vacuole
  • Endoplasmic reticulum
    • Synthesis of lipid — lipid is synthesized in a eukaryotic cell using a chemical in an endoplasmic reticulum. This process requires free-energy.
  • Plastid
    • Autoreplication of chloroplast — a chloroplast is autoreplicate resulting in another chloroplast
    • Photosynthesis — carbon dioxide is converted in a cell to sugar at a chloroplast. This process transforms free-energy and visible light to chemical energy.
    • In carrot plant, starch is stored by an amyloplast in another amyloplast in a carrot and a lateral root
    • amylose is stored by an amyloplast
    • In plant cell, energy is transformed by a chloroplast in a eukaryote to another energy
  • Golgi apparatus
    • A glycoprotein is stored by Golgi apparatus
    • protein is released by Golgi apparatus
    • Distinguishing occurs in Golgi apparatus
    • A chemical is segregated by Golgi apparatus
    • A transport vesicle is produced by Golgi apparatus using an organic molecule
    • Chemical reaction — a chemical is converted to another chemical in Golgi apparatus
  • Lysosome
    • In macrophage, a virus is destroyed by a macrophage in a lysosome
    • Intracellular digestion — a lysosome digests in a eukaryotic cell resulting in an organic molecule
    • debris is degraded by a lysosomal enzyme in a eukaryotic cell at a lysosome
