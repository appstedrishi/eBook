Chemiosmosis

chemiosmosis

An energy-coupling mechanism that uses energy stored in the form of a hydrogen ion gradient across a membrane to drive cellular work, such as the synthesis of ATP. Most ATP synthesis in cells occurs by chemiosmosis.

Definition of a chemiosmosis

An energy-coupling mechanism that uses energy stored in the form of a hydrogen ion gradient across a membrane to drive cellular work, such as the synthesis of ATP. Most ATP synthesis in cells occurs by chemiosmosis.

Chemiosmosis is a type of: energy coupling.

Participants of a chemiosmosis

An ADP and an inorganic phosphate are converted by an ATP synthase with a hydrogen ion gradient to ATP

Steps of chemiosmosis

  • Diffusion by a chemical and a hydrogen ion by means of a concentration gradient to a hypotonic solution with a transport protein from a hypertonic solution through a biomembrane using a membrane potential. This process transforms a proton-motive force to free-energy.
  • Activation of ATP synthase — an ATP synthase is activated resulting in another ATP synthase
  • Synthesis of ATP — an ADP and an inorganic phosphate are converted in a cell to ATP in an active site. This process transforms activation energy and free-energy to another free-energy.
