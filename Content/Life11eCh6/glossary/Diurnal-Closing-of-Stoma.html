Diurnal closing of stoma

diurnal closing of stoma

Stomata are openings in a plant's leaves that allow for gas exchange between the plant and the atmosphere. The plants close their stomata daily in response to light, carbon dioxide depletion, and an internal "clock" in guard cells.
